A handful of wineries are global icons — through history, scarcity, or a wine
so good it became a benchmark. These are the names even non-drinkers half
recognise.

## Domaine de la Romanée-Conti, France

The most mythologised wine on Earth — a microscopic Burgundy grand cru whose
bottles change hands for the price of a car. Visiting is essentially
impossible; the vineyard wall is the pilgrimage.

## Château Lafite Rothschild, France

The Pauillac first growth whose name became shorthand for the summit of
Bordeaux.

## Château d'Yquem, France

The undisputed king of sweet wine, made on a Sauternes hill for centuries.

## Penfolds Grange, Australia

The wine that proved the New World could make something with decades of
ageing — Australia's most famous bottle.

## Vega Sicilia, Spain

Spain's legendary estate, whose Único is released only after a decade-plus in
the cellar.

## Sassicaia / Tenuta San Guido, Italy

The "Super Tuscan" that broke the rules and created a category.

## Krug & Dom Pérignon, France

The Champagne houses whose names are synonyms for luxury fizz worldwide.

## Screaming Eagle, USA

The Napa "cult cab" whose tiny production and waiting list made it a legend.

## Château Mouton Rothschild, France

First growth famous for its artist labels — Picasso, Warhol, Bacon — and its
museum.

## Antinori, Italy

A 600-year wine dynasty whose modern Chianti winery is a destination in itself.

## What "famous" actually means

Scarcity drives the myth (Romanée-Conti, Screaming Eagle), history confers
authority (Antinori, Lafite), and one transcendent wine can make a name
(Grange, Sassicaia). Fame rarely means you can buy — or even visit — but it
marks the estates that set the standards everyone else chases.

## Visiting the icons

Reality check: the most famous estates are the least visitable. Romanée-Conti
and the Bordeaux first growths rarely take casual visitors; cult Napa
producers are allocation-only. The accessible icons are the great Champagne
houses (Moët, Mellon — bookable cellar tours) and showcase estates like
Antinori. Visit the legends' neighbours and the négociant houses; book months
ahead for any that do open.
