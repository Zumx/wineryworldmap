Argentina is the world's fifth-largest wine producer and the uncontested homeland of Malbec at altitude. The Mendoza region, with vineyards ranging from 900 to 1,500 metres above sea level, produces reds of a depth, colour, and ripeness impossible at European latitudes. The Uco Valley sub-region — Valle de Uco, at 1,000 to 1,500 metres, with significant diurnal temperature variation — has become the frontier for Argentina's most ambitious wine projects over the past two decades. These ten estates lead the country.

## 1. Catena Zapata, Mendoza

Nicolás Catena Zapata is the single figure most responsible for Argentina's fine wine revolution. His family estate, based in the Agrelo district of Luján de Cuyo, makes Adrianna Vineyard Malbec from a single site at 1,450 metres in the Uco Valley — widely considered Argentina's greatest single vineyard. The Adrianna series (Mundus Bacillus Terrae, White Bones, River Stones) explores different parcels within the estate with scientific precision. The Nicolás Catena Zapata blend — Malbec and Cabernet Sauvignon — is the iconic multi-varietal flagship. Catena also owns Caro (a collaboration with Château Lafite Rothschild) and produces the widely distributed Catena and Alamos labels. Winery tours operate from the striking Mayan pyramid-inspired building in Agrelo.

## 2. Bodega Norton, Mendoza

One of Argentina's oldest and most respected estates, founded by English engineer Edmund James Palmer Norton in 1895, now owned by the Austrian Swarovski family. Norton's flagship Privada is a blend of Malbec, Merlot, and Cabernet Sauvignon that is one of the most consistent premium reds in Mendoza. The single-vineyard Pedriel Malbec, from low-yielding old vines in Luján de Cuyo, and the Barrel Select range represent the estate's quality range. Norton's large winery and visitor centre in Perdriel receive substantial numbers of wine tourists and offer one of the most polished experiences in Mendoza.

## 3. Achaval-Ferrer, Mendoza

Founded in 1998 by Roberto Cipresso and Santiago Achaval, this Luján de Cuyo estate quickly established itself as a benchmark for single-vineyard Malbec expression. The three flagship wines — Finca Bella Vista, Finca Mirador, and Finca Altamira — each express a distinct terroir in Luján de Cuyo and Uco Valley respectively, with Altamira from a 1,000-metre elevation limestone-rich site often cited as among Argentina's finest. The Quimera blend (Malbec, Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc) is the multi-varietal flagship. The estate was acquired by Clos de los Siete in recent years but maintains its quality focus.

## 4. Susana Balbo Wines, Mendoza

Susana Balbo, Argentina's first female winemaker to earn an oenology degree, established her eponymous estate in Agrelo and has become one of the country's most prominent wine personalities. Her Brioso blend (Cabernet Franc dominant) is the prestige flagship; the Signature series covers single-varietal Malbec, Torrontés, and Cabernet Sauvignon. The estate is also known for its Crios label (widely distributed entry range) and for pioneering quality Torrontés from the high-altitude Calchaquí Valleys of Salta, where the variety achieves its most fragrant, precise expression at 1,700 metres.

## 5. Bodega Salentein, Uco Valley

A Dutch-owned estate in the Valle de Uco at 1,050 to 1,200 metres elevation, Salentein is one of the largest and most architecturally striking wineries in Argentina — the cruciform underground cellar by architect Bórmida & Yanzón is a landmark. The Primus range (single-vineyard Malbec, Pinot Noir, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Chardonnay) represents the estate's top tier, with Pinot Noir from the cool Valle de Uco particularly noteworthy. The Numina Gran Corte is the multi-varietal flagship blend. Salentein's visitor facilities include the Posada Salentein hotel and a contemporary art museum within the winery complex.

## 6. Trapiche, Mendoza

Argentina's largest exporter and one of its most recognisable names internationally, Trapiche was founded in 1883 and operates from a historic winery in Godoy Cruz. Quality has risen sharply in the premium tiers: the Ísay single-vineyard series covers distinct Mendoza terroirs; the Medalla range and Terroir Series Malbec represent reliable premium expressions. At the apex, the ISCAY range (Malbec-Merlot and Malbec-Cabernet Franc blends) and the Fond de Cave bottlings demonstrate what Trapiche's vineyards can produce at maximum ambition. The recently released Gran Medalla Malbec from the Luján de Cuyo estate has won significant international recognition.

## 7. Bodega Caro, Mendoza

Bodega Caro is the joint venture between Catena Zapata and Château Lafite Rothschild, launched in 2000. The flagship Caro blend (Malbec and Cabernet Sauvignon, with the Malbec dominant) is styled with a Bordeaux-influenced precision that distinguishes it from the richer, fruitier mainstream of Mendoza. The Amancaya second wine (Malbec-Cabernet Sauvignon) is widely available. Aruma, the entry-level Malbec, offers the joint-venture quality at an accessible price. The combination of Catena's Adrianna and Nicasia vineyard fruit and Lafite's technical consultation produces wines of notable elegance for the region.

## 8. Zuccardi Valle de Uco, Mendoza

The Zuccardi family estate has become Argentina's most discussed winery of the past decade, following José Alberto Zuccardi and his son Sebastián's decision to focus entirely on high-altitude Valle de Uco expression. The Zuccardi Valle de Uco winery, opened in 2016 and winner of the World's Best Winery award in 2019, is built directly into the alluvial fan at Paraje Altamira. The Piedra Infinita Malbec (single parcel, 1,000 metres) and Concreto Malbec (fermented in concrete vessels without oak) are the prestige expressions. The Alluvional series explores distinct soil types within the Uco Valley. Zuccardi's commitment to mapping Mendoza's geological diversity has generated the most rigorous terroir study of any Argentine producer.

## 9. Terrazas de los Andes, Mendoza

The LVMH-owned estate in Luján de Cuyo, established in 1999 from former Chandon Argentina vineyard holdings, has emerged as one of Mendoza's most ambitious fine wine producers. The single-vineyard Las Compuertas Malbec (old vines, Luján de Cuyo) and Parcel Los Cerros Malbec (Uco Valley) are the prestige expressions. The Gran Malbec and Gran Cabernet Sauvignon represent reliable premium quality, and the estate's collaboration with Cheval Blanc (Cheval des Andes — a Malbec-Cabernet Franc blend) is among Argentina's most sought-after wines.

## 10. Bodega Aleanna, Mendoza

Founded in 2003 by Alejandro Vigil — also the chief winemaker at Catena Zapata — Aleanna is a small personal project focused on single-vineyard and single-parcel Malbec from extreme-altitude vineyards. El Enemigo (The Enemy) is the primary label; Gran Enemigo is the prestige single-vineyard tier, with the Gualtallary and Agrelo parcels the most acclaimed. Bodega Aleanna represents the direction of Argentina's most talented winemakers: small scale, site-specific, and technically rigorous in a way that the large commercial estates cannot replicate.

## High Altitude as a Style Marker

The unifying characteristic of Argentina's best Malbec is altitude. Luján de Cuyo's vineyards at 900 to 1,100 metres produce wines of deep colour, plum and dark chocolate concentration, and moderate acidity. Uco Valley vineyards at 1,000 to 1,500 metres add significant freshness, mineral tension, and violet fragrance — a result of the larger diurnal temperature range at altitude. The best Argentine Malbec rewards cellaring for five to fifteen years; the single-vineyard expressions from Catena, Zuccardi, and Achaval-Ferrer rival the world's great reds for quality and longevity.
