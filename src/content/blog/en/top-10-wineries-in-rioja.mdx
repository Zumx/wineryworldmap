Rioja is Spain's most internationally recognised wine region. Tempranillo-
led blends aged in American (and increasingly French) oak, classified by
ageing — Crianza, Reserva, Gran Reserva — that puts time in barrel and
bottle on the label. The ten estates below are the names that built that
reputation.

## 1. R. López de Heredia (Viña Tondonia), Haro

The most uncompromising classical producer in Rioja. Viña Tondonia
Reserva, Viña Bosconia, and white Tondonia are released only when ready —
sometimes decades after vintage.

## 2. La Rioja Alta, Haro

The Gran Reserva 904 and Gran Reserva 890 are reference points of
classical Rioja Alta — long oak ageing, long bottle ageing, only released
in great vintages.

## 3. CVNE (Compañía Vinícola del Norte de España), Haro

The Imperial Gran Reserva (Rioja Alta) and Viña Real (Alavesa) are
classical Rioja benchmarks; Contino (a separate estate) is the
single-vineyard expression.

## 4. Marqués de Murrieta, Logroño

The historical Rioja estate, with the Castillo Ygay Gran Reserva
Especial — one of the longest-aged commercial wines on Earth, sometimes
held thirty years before release.

## 5. Marqués de Riscal, Elciego

The other founding Rioja Alavesa house, with a Frank Gehry-designed
hotel and visitor complex. The Gran Reserva and Barón de Chirel are the
flagships.

## 6. Bodegas Muga, Haro

A traditional Haro estate making cooperage on site; Prado Enea is the
Gran Reserva flagship and a touchstone of mid-century Rioja style.

## 7. Artadi, Laguardia (Rioja Alavesa)

The leading modern Rioja estate, focused on single-vineyard Tempranillo
from old vines. Viña El Pisón and Pagos Viejos are reference wines.

## 8. Remírez de Ganuza, Samaniego (Rioja Alavesa)

A modern, very high-end producer; Trasnocho and the Gran Reserva are
extraordinarily concentrated Tempranillo expressions.

## 9. Bodegas Roda, Haro

A modern Haro producer; Roda I, Cirsion, and the Gran Reserva are
benchmarks of contemporary Rioja made with old-vine Tempranillo from
named parcels.

## 10. Bodegas Bilbainas, Haro

The historical home of Viña Pomal Reserva, one of the most
recognisable Rioja brands; significant vineyard holdings and a
classical house style.

## Visiting Rioja

Haro and Logroño are the natural bases. The Barrio de la Estación in
Haro — six historic bodegas clustered around the railway station — is
the most concentrated wine-tourism walk in Spain. Most estates take
appointments; some run shop-front tastings.
