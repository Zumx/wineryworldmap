Oregon's Willamette Valley runs south of Portland and is the New
World's most serious Pinot Noir region. The 1979 World Wine
Olympiad — when David Lett's Eyrie Pinot rivalled Joseph Drouhin's
Chambolle — convinced Burgundy that Oregon could play. These ten
estates have built the case since.

## 1. The Eyrie Vineyards, Dundee Hills

The founding estate. David Lett planted the first Pinot Noir in
Oregon in 1965 and his son Jason continues with a delicate,
Burgundian-leaning house style.

## 2. Domaine Drouhin Oregon, Dundee Hills

Joseph Drouhin's Oregon outpost, established 1987 — the moment a
classed Burgundy house bet on the New World. Cuvée Laurène is the
flagship Pinot.

## 3. Domaine Serene, Dundee Hills

A leading Dundee Hills estate with a high-end visitor programme;
Evenstad Reserve and Mark Bradford Vineyard are reference Oregon
Pinot Noirs.

## 4. Bergström Wines, Newberg (Chehalem Mountains)

A biodynamic Newberg estate; the Sigrid Chardonnay and Bergström
Vineyard Pinot Noir are top expressions of the new Oregon style.

## 5. Cristom Vineyards, Eola-Amity Hills

A leading Eola-Amity estate, with whole-cluster Pinot Noir from
named sites — Jessie, Eileen, Marjorie, Louise — set the modern
Oregon standard.

## 6. Beaux Frères, Newberg

The estate co-founded by Robert Parker's brother-in-law; one of
the early premium-Oregon producers and a benchmark Newberg Hills
Pinot.

## 7. Antica Terra, Dundee Hills

A boutique Pinot Noir specialist on jory soils; consistently among
the most highly regarded Oregon producers.

## 8. Adelsheim Vineyard, Newberg (Chehalem Mountains)

One of the historic Willamette founders (1971). The Elizabeth's
Reserve Pinot Noir and the Pinot Gris programme are reference
Oregon wines.

## 9. King Estate, Eugene (South Willamette)

The largest certified-organic vineyard in the United States;
strong on Pinot Gris and Pinot Noir, with a major destination
restaurant and visitor centre.

## 10. Ponzi Vineyards, Sherwood (Laurelwood District)

A founding family estate (1970). Pinot Noir Reserve and Tavola are
the flagships, plus the historic Arneis programme.

## Visiting Willamette

Portland is the gateway; McMinnville, Newberg, and Dundee are the
main wine-tourism hubs. Distances are short, and many estates
welcome the public without appointment. The IPNC (International
Pinot Noir Celebration) in McMinnville each July is the social
peak.
