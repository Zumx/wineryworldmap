Biodynamic viticulture — Rudolf Steiner's 1924 system, now codified
by Demeter — has been adopted by a remarkable number of the world's
top estates. The practice combines organic farming, lunar-calendar
work, and a series of preparations (notably 500 and 501) applied to
the soil and vines. These are the estates most associated with it.

## Burgundy

Domaine Leroy and Domaine Leflaive are the most prestigious
biodynamic names in Burgundy. Domaine Trapet, Lucien Le Moine,
Domaine de la Romanée-Conti (partial), and Comte Armand all
practise biodynamics at the grand-cru level.

## Loire

Nicolas Joly at Coulée de Serrant is the famous proselytiser.
Domaine Huet, Mark Angeli, Olivier Cousin, and Domaine Vacheron all
farm biodynamically.

## Alsace

Domaine Zind-Humbrecht, Domaine Marc Kreydenweiss, Domaine Marcel
Deiss, Domaine Ostertag, and Domaine Josmeyer — among the highest
biodynamic densities of any wine region.

## Northern Rhône

M. Chapoutier was an early Demeter convert at scale. The estate
runs the largest biodynamic vineyard area in France.

## Champagne

Larmandier-Bernier, Vouette et Sorbée, and Agrapart are leading
biodynamic growers; Louis Roederer and Bollinger have biodynamic
parcels within larger holdings.

## Italy

Foradori (Trentino), Castello dei Rampolla (Chianti Classico),
Fontodi (Chianti Classico), Castello di Ama (partial), and La
Stoppa (Emilia) are leading biodynamic Italian estates.

## Spain and Portugal

Telmo Rodríguez's Compañía de Vinos works biodynamically across
multiple appellations; Recaredo in Cava and Niepoort in the Douro
are pioneers.

## New World

Cullen (Margaret River), Gemtree (McLaren Vale), Henschke (Eden
Valley), Felton Road (Central Otago), Seresin (Marlborough),
Reyneke (Stellenbosch), Quivira (Sonoma), Benziger (Sonoma),
Frey (Mendocino), Bonny Doon (Santa Cruz), and Truchard (Carneros)
are reference biodynamic New World estates.

## What does biodynamics change?

There's no consensus on whether biodynamic wine tastes objectively
better. What's clear: biodynamic estates tend to be smaller,
manually farmed, and run by obsessive owners — which may matter as
much as the preparations themselves.
