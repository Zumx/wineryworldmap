Hungary has 22 wine regions and a winemaking history that pre-dates
most of Western Europe's. For decades after the Communist era,
Hungarian wine was associated only with sweet Tokaji and the bulk
Bull's Blood (Egri Bikavér) sold abroad. Since the 1990s a quiet
revolution has rebuilt the regions: serious dry Furmint from
Tokaj, structured Bordeaux-style blends from Villány, volcanic
whites from Somló and Badacsony, and Pinot Noir from Eger. The
estates below are the names to follow.

## Royal Tokaji

Founded in 1990 by Hugh Johnson and others, Royal Tokaji helped
restart the appellation after decades of state ownership. The
Nyúlászó, Szt. Tamás, and Mézes Mály vineyard bottlings of Aszú
6-puttonyos restored the international reputation of Tokaj.

## Disznókő

A first-growth Tokaj estate (one of 28 vineyards classified in
1737) owned by the AXA group. The dry Furmint and the Aszú 5- and
6-puttonyos bottlings are exceptionally consistent.

## István Szepsy

The patriarch of modern dry Furmint. Szepsy redefined what Furmint
could be as a dry table wine, with bottlings from individual
Tokaj vineyards (Úrágya, Király, Birsalmás). The price reflects
the international demand.

## Heimann

A Szekszárd estate run by Zoltán Heimann. The estate has been
particularly influential for Kadarka, the historical Hungarian red
variety, and the Heimann Kadarka has become a benchmark for the
elegant, paler style of the grape.

## Heumann

A Villány estate of Swiss origin, focused on Bordeaux varieties
(Cabernet Franc, Merlot) and Syrah. The wines have a clean,
international precision that has made them export favourites.

## Sauska

Both Tokaj and Villány estates owned by the Sauska family. The
Villány Cabernet Franc is one of Hungary's most respected reds; the
Tokaj sparkling wine and dry Furmint show the breadth of the
group's ambition.

## Vylyan

A Villány estate known for elegant, ageable Cabernet Franc and
Bordeaux blends. The Mandolás Cabernet Franc is the standard-bearer.

## Somlói Apátsági Pince

Reactivated monastic estate on the volcanic cone of Somló in
western Hungary. Somló's signature varieties — Juhfark, Hárslevelű,
Furmint, Olaszrizling — give intense, mineral whites at low
volumes; this estate is among the best.

## Áron

A small estate in Eger producing serious Bikavér (the dry red
appellation blend) and notable Pinot Noir. Eger has emerged in the
last decade as a serious Pinot Noir region — cooler than Villány,
on rhyolite and tuff soils — and Áron is one of the names to know.

## Kreinbacher

A Somló estate that, alongside fine still wines from the volcano,
has built one of Hungary's most respected sparkling-wine houses.
The Brut Classic and the Brut Prestige Riserva are made by the
traditional method and stand comparison with serious Champagne.
