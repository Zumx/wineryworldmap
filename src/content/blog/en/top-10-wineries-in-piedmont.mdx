Piedmont is the home of Nebbiolo, a grape that ripens late, slowly, and
only on south-facing slopes. The Langhe hills around Alba — Barolo,
Barbaresco, and the surrounding crus — concentrate the world's
references for the variety. The ten estates below shape the appellation.

## 1. Gaja, Barbaresco

Angelo Gaja redefined Italian fine wine in the 1970s and 1980s. The
Barbaresco DOCG bottle and the Sorì single-vineyards (Sorì San Lorenzo,
Sorì Tildìn, Costa Russi) are international references.

## 2. Giacomo Conterno, Monforte d'Alba

The traditionalist benchmark of Barolo. Monfortino Riserva is one of
Italy's most age-worthy reds; the Francia, Arione, and Cascina Francia
bottlings are the working portfolio.

## 3. Bruno Giacosa, Neive

A revered house split between Barbaresco and Barolo, with red-label
Riservas only declared in great years. Asili and Santo Stefano in
Barbaresco; Falletto and Rocche del Falletto in Barolo.

## 4. Vietti, Castiglione Falletto

Single-vineyard Barolo from Rocche di Castiglione, Brunate, Lazzarito,
and Villero — a study in Barolo terroir, with iconic artist labels and
strong Barbera bottlings.

## 5. Giuseppe Mascarello, Castiglione Falletto

The Monprivato single-vineyard Barolo from the Michet clone of
Nebbiolo, in the traditional style — long maceration, large old wood;
Riserva Ca' d'Morissio is the rare collector cuvée.

## 6. Roberto Voerzio, La Morra

A modernist La Morra estate of extraordinary intensity; single-vineyard
Barolo Brunate, Cerequio, and Rocche dell'Annunziata at very low yields
and with international reputation.

## 7. Ceretto, Alba

A family estate with holdings in the top Langhe crus — Bricco Rocche in
Castiglione Falletto, Brunate in La Morra — and a striking architectural
programme including the Cube pavilion in the vineyard.

## 8. Produttori del Barbaresco, Barbaresco

The reference cooperative — a co-op of growers in the Barbaresco
commune producing single-vineyard Riservas of remarkable consistency
and value, including Asili, Rabajà, and Montestefano.

## 9. Vajra, Barolo

A family estate in the Vergne hamlet of Barolo, with Bricco delle Viole
as flagship; one of the most respected modern-classical producers, with
a serious Riesling and Freisa as further specialities.

## 10. Pio Cesare, Alba

A historic family house in Alba itself, with vineyards in Ornato (Barolo
Serralunga) and Il Bricco (Barbaresco Treiso); long-aged, classical
Barolo and Barbaresco in roughly equal measure.

## Visiting Piedmont

Alba is the natural base, with Barolo, Barbaresco, and Roero villages
within thirty minutes. Most estates accept appointments; the village
*enoteche* in Barolo and Barbaresco offer multi-producer tastings. Truffle
season (October-November) overlaps with vintage and is peak tourism.
