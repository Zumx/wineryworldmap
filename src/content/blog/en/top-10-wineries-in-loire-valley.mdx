The Loire runs for a thousand kilometres and packs more diversity into its
banks than any other French wine region. Sauvignon Blanc in the east,
Chenin Blanc in the centre, Cabernet Franc on the bend, and Melon de
Bourgogne at the Atlantic mouth. These ten estates are reference points.

## 1. Domaine Didier Dagueneau, Pouilly-Fumé

The defining estate of single-vineyard Sauvignon Blanc — Pur Sang, Silex,
and the legendary Astéroïde from ungrafted vines on flint soil.

## 2. Domaine Henri Bourgeois, Sancerre

A family domaine with deep Sancerre roots, single-vineyard expressions
including Jadis and the Étienne Henri cuvée, plus a sister estate in
Marlborough.

## 3. Domaine Vacheron, Sancerre

The reference for biodynamic Sancerre and Pinot Noir from the same hill;
single-parcel whites and a long-aged Belle Dame red.

## 4. Domaine Huet, Vouvray

The reference of Vouvray — biodynamic Chenin Blanc from Le Mont,
Le Haut-Lieu, and Clos du Bourg in sec, demi-sec, moelleux, and
sparkling.

## 5. Domaine François Pinon, Vouvray

A traditional, biodynamic Chenin estate of great purity; sec and
demi-sec wines that age for decades.

## 6. Domaine Charles Joguet, Chinon

The estate that elevated Chinon to grand status — single-vineyard
Cabernet Franc from Clos de la Dioterie and Clos du Chêne Vert.

## 7. Domaine Couly-Dutheil, Chinon

A long-established Chinon estate with deep tuffeau cellars in the town
itself and the Clos de l'Echo single-vineyard wine as flagship.

## 8. Domaine Nicolas Joly, Savennières

The most famous biodynamic estate in the world. La Coulée de Serrant is
a monopole Chenin Blanc of extraordinary, oxidative, idiosyncratic
character.

## 9. Clos Naudin (Philippe Foreau), Vouvray

A small, classical Vouvray estate making some of the longest-lived
Chenin Blanc in the world across dry, sweet, and sparkling.

## 10. Domaine de l'Ecu, Muscadet

The reference for biodynamic Muscadet — single-vineyard Melon de
Bourgogne from gneiss, granite, and orthogneiss, ageing surprisingly
long on lees.

## Visiting the Loire

The Loire is best driven from west to east, base by base — Nantes for
Muscadet, Angers and Saumur for Chenin and Cabernet Franc, Tours for
Vouvray and Chinon, Sancerre for the east. Many of the historic estates
have cellars cut directly into the tuffeau cliffs along the river.
