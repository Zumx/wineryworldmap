Quinta do Noval is the reference vintage Port estate of the Pinhão
valley in the Douro. Founded in 1715, the estate has produced
declared vintage Ports of legendary status across three centuries —
including the famous "Nacional" bottling from a tiny ungrafted
parcel.

## The estate

Quinta do Noval covers 145 hectares of terraced schist vineyards
above the Pinhão river. The estate was a Port lodge owner-grower
for most of its history; in 1993 it was acquired by AXA Millésimes
(which also owns Pichon Baron in Pauillac and Disznókő in Tokaj).
Christian Seely runs AXA's wine portfolio.

## The Nacional

The Nacional is the single most legendary wine in Port. It comes
from a 2.5-hectare parcel of vines that survived the phylloxera
outbreak of the 1860s and were never grafted onto American
rootstock. The wine has been declared 23 times since 1931. The
1931, 1963, and 1994 are among the most expensive Ports ever sold.

## The other wines

- **Quinta do Noval Vintage Port** — declared in great years. The
2011, 2016, 2017, and 2018 are reference modern Vintage Ports.
- **Quinta do Noval LBV (Late Bottled Vintage)** — released
roughly four years after vintage; a more accessible Port from the
same grapes.
- **Quinta do Noval Aged Tawny** — 10, 20, 30, and 40-year-old
tawny Ports, blended for consistent house style.
- **Quinta do Noval dry Douro red and white** — increasingly
important table wines from the same vineyards.

## The terroir

The estate is in the heart of the Cima Corgo, the central Douro
sub-region considered the best for Port. South-facing schist
terraces, low annual rainfall, very hot summers, and old vines
contribute to the unusually concentrated style.

## Visiting

Quinta do Noval welcomes visitors at the estate above Pinhão.
Tours include the terraced vineyards, the cellar, and a structured
tasting; the Nacional parcel is shown but not poured. Book ahead.
Pinhão is a one-hour drive from Vila Nova de Gaia or a slow train
along the Douro from Porto.
