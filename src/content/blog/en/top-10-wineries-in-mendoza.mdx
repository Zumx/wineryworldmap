Mendoza sits in the Andean foothills at 800 to 1,500 metres above sea
level. Intense sun, cool nights, very low humidity, and snowmelt
irrigation produce Malbec of extraordinary depth — and a new
generation of single-parcel wines that finally treat Argentina as a
serious terroir region.

## 1. Catena Zapata, Luján de Cuyo

The estate that made high-altitude Malbec famous. The Adrianna
Vineyard (1,450 m) is one of the world's most studied vineyards;
White Bones Chardonnay and White Stones Malbec are flagships.

## 2. Zuccardi Valle de Uco, Uco Valley

The most exciting Argentine project of the last decade. Concreto
(Malbec in concrete egg), Finca Piedra Infinita, and the Zeta
ultra-premium Malbec define the new Uco Valley style.

## 3. Achaval-Ferrer, Luján de Cuyo / Uco Valley

A modern producer focused on single-vineyard Malbec. Finca Bella
Vista, Finca Altamira, and Finca Mirador are reference parcellaires.

## 4. Bodega Salentein, Uco Valley

A Dutch-owned estate built at 1,050-1,500 m in the Uco Valley; an
ambitious cellar designed as a walk-in artwork and an excellent
restaurant programme.

## 5. Bodega Aleanna (El Enemigo), Uco Valley

Adrianna Catena and David Bonomi's biodynamic estate from old vines
in cool Gualtallary. El Enemigo Malbec and Gran Enemigo Cabernet
Franc are benchmarks of Argentine finesse.

## 6. Terrazas de los Andes, Luján de Cuyo

The LVMH Argentina project, organising vineyards by altitude
terraces. Joint venture Cheval des Andes (with Cheval Blanc) is the
prestige cuvée.

## 7. Bodega Norton, Luján de Cuyo

A historic English-founded estate (1895) now Swarovski-owned;
Privada and Perdriel Malbec are reference wines and the visitor
programme is among the best.

## 8. Susana Balbo Wines, Agrelo

Argentina's first female enology graduate and one of its most
influential winemakers. Strong on Torrontés as well as Malbec;
BenMarco is the export flagship.

## 9. Bodega Caro, Mendoza

The joint venture between Lafite Rothschild and Catena Zapata.
Caro and Amancaya are Bordeaux-meets-Mendoza Malbec-Cabernet
blends.

## 10. Bodega Chacra (technically Patagonia)

Worth a mention even outside Mendoza: Piero Incisa della
Rocchetta's biodynamic estate on ungrafted 1930s Pinot Noir vines —
the new Argentine Pinot benchmark.

## Visiting Mendoza

Most estates are within an hour of Mendoza city. The Uco Valley
requires a further hour south but rewards the trip with the highest
sites and coolest terroirs. Bike tours work well in Luján de Cuyo;
4×4 tours reach the highest Gualtallary plots. Peak season is March
to May (harvest and autumn).
