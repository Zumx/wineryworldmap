Opus One is the joint venture between Robert Mondavi (the late
patriarch of California fine wine) and Baron Philippe de Rothschild
of Château Mouton Rothschild, founded in 1979 and producing a
single wine: a Bordeaux-style blend, predominantly Cabernet
Sauvignon, made in Oakville, Napa Valley. The estate was the first
of its kind — a transatlantic 50-50 partnership between an
American and a French First Growth — and helped legitimise Napa as
a peer of Bordeaux for serious collectors.

## The partnership and origins

Mondavi and Rothschild met at the 1970 Hawaii wine tasting and
discussed a collaboration. In 1979 they made the first vintage
jointly; a year later, the partnership was formalised and named
Opus One — the Latin musical term for "first work", an
appropriately ambitious title. The Mondavi family sold its stake
to Constellation Brands in 2004; the Rothschild family retains its
50% share.

## The vineyards

Opus One owns 170 acres in three vineyards: the To-Kalon vineyard
section in Oakville (the most celebrated parcel, shared with
Robert Mondavi Winery), the Q-Block, and the BV-Q. The blend is
dominated by Cabernet Sauvignon (typically 80%+) with smaller
percentages of Petit Verdot, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, and
Malbec — the classic five Bordeaux red varieties.

## The winemaking

The Opus One winery, completed in 1991 and designed by
architects Johnson Fain, is a stark, half-buried semicircular
structure that has become one of the most recognisable winery
buildings in California. Inside, fermentation takes place in
small stainless steel tanks, allowing parcel-by-parcel
vinification; the wine is then aged for 18 months in 100% new
French oak. The barrel cellar is a vaulted, columned space
deliberately reminiscent of a Bordeaux first-growth chai.

## The wine

Opus One is consistently among the most expensive single Napa
wines, with current-release pricing of $400-500 per bottle. The
style sits between Bordeaux and California: more structured and
restrained than the typical Napa Cabernet of the 2000s and 2010s,
with the polish and oak signature of fine Bordeaux. Recent
vintages have benefitted from a stylistic move toward greater
freshness; older bottles (1980s-1990s) show a slightly more
Bordeaux-style austerity.

## Second wine: Overture

Opus One also produces a second wine, Overture, which sources from
the same vineyards and uses the same winemaking but with grapes
deemed not quite suited to the grand vin. Overture is not produced
in every vintage; it is bottled when sufficient quality material
is available.

## The cultural significance

Opus One arrived at a critical moment in the international
wine market. The Judgment of Paris (1976) had shown that
California could match Bordeaux in blind tasting; Opus One in
1979 brought a Bordeaux first-growth proprietor to Napa
permanently. Within a decade, multiple other French-American
partnerships followed (Dominus by Christian Moueix, who managed
Cheval Blanc; Bryant Family etc.).

## Ageing potential

Top Opus One vintages develop over 20 to 30 years. The 1991
and 2002 bottlings have a strong following; the 2015 and 2018
recent vintages have been well-rated. Older bottles benefit from
careful storage and reach their peak between 15 and 25 years
from release.

## Visiting

Opus One offers visitors a "Premiere" tasting by appointment.
The visit includes the rooftop terrace overlooking the Mayacamas
range and a tasting of the current vintage and one library
selection. The building is one of the most architecturally
significant winery visits in California.
