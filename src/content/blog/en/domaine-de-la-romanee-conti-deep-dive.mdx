Domaine de la Romanée-Conti — universally known as DRC — sits in
Vosne-Romanée on the Côte de Nuits. It is the single most legendary
estate in wine. Its holdings are tiny, its prices astronomical, and
its presence in any wine list is a statement on the part of the
restaurant.

## The holdings

DRC owns or co-manages seven grand cru parcels:
- **Romanée-Conti** (monopole, 1.8 hectares)
- **La Tâche** (monopole, 6.06 hectares)
- **Richebourg** (3.51 hectares)
- **Romanée-Saint-Vivant** (5.29 hectares)
- **Grands-Échezeaux** (3.53 hectares)
- **Échezeaux** (4.67 hectares)
- **Montrachet** (0.67 hectares — the white)
- **Corton** (since 2009)

All in Vosne-Romanée except Montrachet and Corton.

## Ownership

DRC is owned jointly by two families — de Villaine and Leroy/Roch.
After Lalou Bize-Leroy left the directorship in 1992 (to focus on
her own Domaine Leroy), Aubert de Villaine ran the estate until 2024;
his nephew Bertrand de Villaine and Henry-Frédéric Roch took over,
with Roch handling viticulture.

## Viticulture

DRC has been certified biodynamic since 2007. Vineyards are
ploughed by horse; harvest is by hand at low yields; the wines are
unfiltered. The estate uses 100% new oak only for Romanée-Conti and
La Tâche; the others see proportionally less.

## The wines

A typical DRC release includes around 6,000 bottles of
Romanée-Conti, 17,000 of La Tâche, 12,000 of Richebourg, 16,000 of
Romanée-Saint-Vivant, 11,000 of Grands-Échezeaux, 16,000 of
Échezeaux, and 3,000 of Montrachet. The estate sells in cases of 13
or 14 bottles — one of each wine.

## Prices

A single bottle of Romanée-Conti can fetch 20,000-40,000 euros at
auction. The 1990 vintage of Romanée-Conti is among the most
expensive standard-sized bottles ever sold; older vintages (1945,
1937) trade for far more.

## Visiting

DRC does not accept casual visitors. Even trade visits are rare and
strictly by referral. The estate's modest gate in Vosne-Romanée
village is well known to pilgrims; the famous stone cross at the
top of the Romanée-Conti monopole is the closest most visitors will
get.
