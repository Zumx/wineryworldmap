The Barossa Valley, an hour northeast of Adelaide, was settled by
Lutheran Germans in the 1840s. Their ungrafted Shiraz vines — some
now over 150 years old — make this the world's most important
reservoir of pre-phylloxera Shiraz. The ten estates below define the
modern Barossa.

## 1. Penfolds, Barossa Valley / Adelaide

The Australian benchmark. Grange — a multi-region Shiraz first made
in 1951 — remains the country's most prestigious red. Bin 707
(Cabernet) and the RWT Shiraz are the other Penfolds icons.

## 2. Henschke (Eden Valley, technically a neighbour but inseparable from Barossa)

Hill of Grace — a single block of Shiraz planted in the 1860s — is
one of the world's great wines. Mount Edelstone is the other estate
flagship; family-owned since the 1860s.

## 3. Torbreck, Marananga (Barossa)

The benchmark of modern Barossa Shiraz. The Laird (a single-vineyard
Shiraz from a 1958 planting) and the Run Rig (Shiraz with a touch of
Viognier) are reference wines.

## 4. Yalumba, Angaston (Barossa)

Australia's oldest family-owned winery (1849). The Octavius Shiraz
and the Caley (Cabernet-Shiraz blend) are the prestige bottlings;
the Old Vine Viognier programme set a New World benchmark.

## 5. Two Hands, Marananga (Barossa)

A modern Barossa specialist with a regional-comparison Shiraz
programme (Bella's Garden, Lily's Garden, Sophie's Garden, etc.)
that maps the appellation through one variety.

## 6. Charles Melton, Tanunda (Barossa)

A traditional Barossa estate with the iconic Nine Popes (Shiraz-
Grenache-Mataro) Rhône-style blend, plus single-vineyard old-vine
Grenache.

## 7. Rockford, Tanunda (Barossa)

A traditional Barossa estate known for the Basket Press Shiraz —
genuinely made in a basket press — and the Black Shiraz (sparkling),
a Barossa cult.

## 8. St Hallett, Tanunda (Barossa)

Producer of Old Block Shiraz, one of the original single-vineyard
old-vine Barossa Shirazes; a benchmark of the appellation.

## 9. Glaetzer, Tanunda (Barossa)

A modernist Barossa estate; Amon-Ra is one of the most acclaimed
Barossa Shirazes of the last two decades.

## 10. Seppeltsfield, Seppeltsfield (Barossa)

A historic estate famous for its Para Tawny barrel hall — fortified
wines from every vintage back to 1878 are still in barrel, and a
100-year-old release is the prestige experience.

## Visiting the Barossa

The Barossa is the most welcoming Australian wine region. Tanunda,
Nuriootpa, and Angaston anchor the valley; most cellar doors open
daily without appointment. Old-vine vineyards are marked on roadside
plaques; many estates offer paired food and old-vine tours.
