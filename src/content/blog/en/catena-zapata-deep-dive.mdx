Bodega Catena Zapata is the estate that, more than any other,
established Argentina as a serious fine wine producer in the
1990s. Nicolás Catena, who took over the family company from his
Italian-immigrant father, made several decisions that reshaped
Argentine viticulture: he focused single-mindedly on Malbec when
the international trade still considered the variety a curiosity,
he pushed vineyards to dramatically higher altitudes than
anyone else in Mendoza had attempted, and he invested in
international-style winemaking equipment and consulting.

## The pyramid winery

The Catena Zapata winery, completed in 2001, is a stepped Mayan
pyramid structure rising from the vineyards at the foot of the
Andes. It has become one of the most photographed wineries in the
southern hemisphere. The interior houses fermentation and ageing
facilities below ground for natural temperature control.

## High-altitude viticulture

When Nicolás Catena began moving Malbec vineyards above 1,500
metres, the prevailing Mendoza assumption was that altitude
brought only cold and frost. Catena's research showed instead
that high-altitude UV exposure thickened the grape skins and
enhanced colour and anthocyanin development. The estate's
Adrianna Vineyard, at 1,500 metres in the Gualtallary area of the
Uco Valley, has produced some of the most highly rated
Argentinean wines.

## Adrianna Vineyard

Named after Nicolás Catena's daughter, the Adrianna Vineyard is
divided into multiple parcels, each bottled separately when
quality warrants. The wines — including Adrianna River Stones,
Adrianna White Stones (Chardonnay), Adrianna Mundus Bacillus
Terrae, and Adrianna Fortuna Terrae — have been at the centre of
Argentina's claim to be capable of producing world-class wine.

## Catena Zapata Argentino

The flagship blend. A Bordeaux-style cuvée (Cabernet
Sauvignon-dominant historically, though more recent vintages have
included Malbec) released only in exceptional vintages. Argentino
demonstrated that Argentine Cabernet could stand alongside Malbec.

## The Catena Institute of Wine

In 1995 the estate founded the Catena Institute of Wine, a
research body led by Nicolás Catena's daughter Laura Catena
(who is also an emergency-room physician in San Francisco). The
institute has published more peer-reviewed research on Mendoza
viticulture than any other organisation and has trained dozens of
younger Argentine winemakers.

## The family lineage

Nicolás Catena now operates with the next generation: Laura
Catena and her cousin Adrianna's son. Daughter Laura's
sister-in-law oversees Bodega Caro, a joint venture with Domaine
Barons de Rothschild (Lafite). The family controls about half a
dozen related Argentine wineries under different labels.

## Beyond Catena Zapata

The Catena range covers multiple price tiers — Catena Alta
single-vineyard, the Catena standard range, the Vista Flores
range, and the parcel-level Adrianna and Nicasia bottlings.
Argentina's other top producers — Achaval-Ferrer, Bodega Aleanna,
Bodega Chacra (Patagonia), and Susana Balbo — owe much of their
international visibility to the path Catena cut.

## Visiting

The Catena Zapata winery accepts visitors by appointment. The
pyramid is open for tours including the underground barrel
cellar, the laboratory, and the tasting room overlooking the
Andes. Mendoza's Maipú and Uco Valley wine regions surround the
estate; many other major producers offer comparable tours within
30 minutes' drive.
