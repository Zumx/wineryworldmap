The Pfalz stretches along the eastern flank of the Haardt mountains, a
mild strip of southern Germany where almond trees flower in March and
fig leaves overhang the cellar doors of Mittelhaardt villages. It is
Germany's second-largest wine region by area and its most consistent
source of powerful dry Riesling. The towns of Forst, Deidesheim,
Ruppertsberg, and Wachenheim form the heart of what Hugh Johnson once
called "the Côte d'Or of Germany."

## Reichsrat von Buhl

A historic Forst estate, once supplier to Bismarck's court, restored
to top form under biodynamic conversion in the last decade. Their
Forster Kirchenstück and Pechstein bottlings are among the most
precise dry Rieslings in Germany.

## Dr. Bürklin-Wolf

Bell Beth Bürklin's estate covers 86 hectares across the great
Mittelhaardt grand crus. The vineyard hierarchy follows a Burgundian
PC/GC system and the wines have a chiselled, mineral, lifetime-ageing
profile.

## Geheimer Rat Dr. von Bassermann-Jordan

A Deidesheim estate that pioneered single-vineyard bottlings in
Germany in the 19th century. The Hohenmorgen and Kalkofen parcels in
Deidesheim produce some of the most savoury Rieslings of the region.

## Müller-Catoir

Based in Haardt, the estate is famous for Rieslaner, Scheurebe, and
Muskateller alongside Riesling — a defender of aromatic varieties
when the rest of the Pfalz went all-in on Riesling.

## Knipser

Based in Laumersheim in the northern Pfalz, the Knipser brothers are
known for Bordeaux blends and Spätburgunder alongside Riesling. Their
"X" cuvée is a benchmark for serious German red.

## Christmann

Steffen Christmann runs both his family estate in Gimmeldingen and the
VDP (Germany's premier wine association). Biodynamic, with
fine-grained Rieslings from the Idig and Mandelgarten parcels.

## Weingut Ökonomierat Rebholz

Hansjörg Rebholz makes electric Riesling and Weissburgunder in the
Südpfalz from limestone soils. The Im Sonnenschein parcels deliver
extraordinarily salty, mineral Riesling.

## Weingut A. Christmann

Not to be confused with VDP-president Steffen — distinct family of
estates, both producing terroir-driven Riesling from Königsbach and
Gimmeldingen vineyards.

## Friedrich Becker

A red specialist in the Südpfalz, Becker's Pinot Noir from
limestone soils across the French border (where his oldest vineyards
sit) has been a benchmark German Pinot for thirty years.

## Weingut Ökonomierat Rebholz (Südpfalz)

Riesling, Weissburgunder, and Spätburgunder from the limestone of
Birkweiler and Siebeldingen. Long considered the most precise
Südpfalz address.

## Planning a Pfalz trip

The Deutsche Weinstrasse runs the length of the region; Forst and
Deidesheim are walkable from one to the other, and most estates open
their Vinothek on weekdays for casual tasting. Find them all on the
[interactive map](/map) and filter to the Pfalz.
