Ribera del Duero sits on a high plateau in Castilla y León, where
Tempranillo (locally Tinto Fino) ripens slowly under intense sun and cold
nights. The DO was only created in 1982, but it now contests Rioja for
the crown of Spanish red wine. These ten estates lead the field.

## 1. Vega Sicilia, Valbuena de Duero

The historic Ribera estate, founded 1864. Único (long-aged Tempranillo
with a touch of Cabernet) and Reserva Especial (a multi-vintage blend)
are Spain's most prestigious reds.

## 2. Dominio de Pingus, Quintanilla de Onésimo

Peter Sisseck's tiny cult estate, founded 1995. Pingus from very old
Tempranillo vines is one of the most expensive Spanish wines; Flor de
Pingus and PSI follow.

## 3. Bodegas Aalto, Quintanilla de Arriba

A modern Ribera estate founded by the former Vega Sicilia winemaker
Mariano García; Aalto and Aalto PS are reference bottlings.

## 4. Tinto Pesquera (Alejandro Fernández), Pesquera de Duero

The estate that put Ribera del Duero on the modern map. Pesquera
Crianza, Reserva, and Janus Gran Reserva remain Spanish classics.

## 5. Bodegas Emilio Moro, Pesquera de Duero

A family estate with a strong line from Resalso (entry) to Malleolus de
Sanchomartín (single-vineyard); one of the best visitor experiences in
the appellation.

## 6. Vega Sicilia – Alión, Peñafiel

The Vega Sicilia group's modern Ribera label — Tempranillo aged in
French oak rather than American — a sleek modern counterpoint to Único.

## 7. Hacienda Monasterio, Pesquera de Duero

A Sisseck-consulted estate (and his original Ribera workplace);
Monasterio Reserva is a benchmark of modern Ribera.

## 8. Bodegas Mauro, Tudela de Duero (technically Castilla y León VT)

Mariano García's other estate, just outside the DO boundary;
Terreus and the Mauro VS bottling are reference wines.

## 9. Bodegas Astrales, Anguix

A high-quality modern Ribera producer; the single bottling Astrales
is a single-village expression of old-vine Tinto Fino.

## 10. Pago de Carraovejas, Peñafiel

A large modern Ribera estate with significant vineyard holdings, a
Michelin-starred restaurant on site, and an unusually polished visitor
experience.

## Visiting Ribera del Duero

Valladolid is the closest large city; Peñafiel — with its iconic
castle housing the Museum of Provincial Wine — is the heart of the
DO and the most practical base. Many estates run scheduled tours;
Pingus and Vega Sicilia do not accept casual visits.
