The Rhône splits into two distinct wine worlds. The Northern Rhône, narrow
and granitic, is Syrah country — Hermitage, Côte-Rôtie, Cornas. The
Southern Rhône, broad and stony, is Grenache-led blend country, headed by
Châteauneuf-du-Pape.

## 1. M. Chapoutier, Tain-l'Hermitage

The largest landholder on the Hermitage hill and a serious biodynamic
producer. Le Pavillon, Le Méal, and L'Ermite are the great single-parcel
Hermitages.

## 2. Domaine Jean-Louis Chave, Mauves

The reference estate of Hermitage — a multi-vineyard blend of Syrah from
the historic *climats* of the hill, made in a long-lived classical style.

## 3. E. Guigal, Ampuis

The Côte-Rôtie reference: the famous La La trio — La Mouline, La Landonne,
La Turque — single-vineyard Syrahs that helped restore Côte-Rôtie's
international reputation.

## 4. Domaine René Rostaing, Côte-Rôtie

A leading Côte-Rôtie family domaine; the La Landonne and Côte Blonde
single-vineyard wines are touchstones of the appellation.

## 5. Domaine Auguste Clape, Cornas

The benchmark of traditional Cornas — old-vine Syrah from steep granite,
long maceration, large old wood. Wines that age for decades.

## 6. Château de Beaucastel, Châteauneuf-du-Pape

The southern Rhône reference, blending all 13 permitted varieties on a
*galets roulés* plateau; an early pioneer of organic and biodynamic
viticulture.

## 7. Château Rayas, Châteauneuf-du-Pape

A cult Châteauneuf made almost entirely from old-vine Grenache on a
sandy parcel; ethereal, perfumed, and impossible to visit casually.

## 8. Domaine du Vieux Télégraphe, Châteauneuf-du-Pape

The Brunier family's estate on La Crau plateau — one of the most
consistent and structured of all Châteauneufs.

## 9. Domaine du Pégaü, Châteauneuf-du-Pape

A traditional Châteauneuf estate famous for the Cuvée Réservée and the
extraordinary, rarely released Cuvée da Capo.

## 10. Domaine Ostertag's neighbour — actually Domaine Tardieu-Laurent, Lourmarin

A boutique négociant assembling old-vine parcels across the Northern and
Southern Rhône; their Côte-Rôtie Vieilles Vignes and Châteauneuf
Spéciale are reference cuvées.

## Visiting the Rhône

The Northern Rhône is a narrow ribbon along the river — Tain, Ampuis,
Cornas — and walkable in places. The Southern Rhône is broader and
car-bound; Châteauneuf village itself concentrates several tasting rooms.
Hermitage and Côte-Rôtie hill walks are open to the public.
