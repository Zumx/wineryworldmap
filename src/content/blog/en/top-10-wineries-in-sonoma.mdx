Sonoma County sits west of Napa, stretching from the Pacific coast
inland over a dozen AVAs. Pacific fog cools the Russian River Valley
and Sonoma Coast; Dry Creek and Alexander Valley get the warmer
afternoons. The result is a broader varietal palette than Napa and a
quieter, less corporate visitor culture.

## 1. Williams Selyem, Healdsburg (Russian River Valley)

The benchmark for Russian River Pinot Noir. Mailing-list allocation
and single-vineyard releases (Rochioli, Allen, Hirsch) of cult status.

## 2. Kistler Vineyards, Sonoma

The reference for site-driven California Chardonnay (Hudson, Stone
Flat, Vine Hill); also a serious Pinot Noir programme.

## 3. Hirsch Vineyards, Cazadero (Sonoma Coast)

A pioneer of the true Sonoma Coast — far-west, fog-soaked Pinot Noir
from one of California's most extreme cool-climate sites.

## 4. Ridge Vineyards (Lytton Springs), Healdsburg (Dry Creek Valley)

The Sonoma half of the legendary Ridge operation. Lytton Springs and
Geyserville are old-vine Zinfandel field-blends of reference quality.

## 5. Rochioli, Healdsburg (Russian River Valley)

A family estate whose Pinot Noir and Sauvignon Blanc helped establish
the Russian River Valley as a Pinot region.

## 6. Jordan Vineyard & Winery, Healdsburg (Alexander Valley)

A Bordeaux-style Alexander Valley estate with a chateau-style visitor
experience and decades-deep cellar; Cabernet Sauvignon is the
flagship.

## 7. Iron Horse Vineyards, Sebastopol (Green Valley)

The leading Sonoma sparkling wine house. Méthode champenoise from
Russian River Pinot Noir and Chardonnay; served at White House state
dinners for decades.

## 8. Marimar Estate, Sebastopol (Russian River Valley)

A family estate founded by Marimar Torres (of Spain's Torres family)
focused on organic Pinot Noir and Chardonnay from cool-climate
Sonoma sites.

## 9. Dutton-Goldfield, Sebastopol (Russian River Valley)

A Pinot Noir-focused Russian River Valley producer with strong
single-vineyard bottlings from Dutton Ranch sites.

## 10. Gundlach Bundschu, Sonoma (Sonoma Valley)

California's oldest continuously family-operated winery (1858), in
the southern Sonoma Valley. Strong on Merlot and Pinot Noir from the
Rhinefarm estate.

## Visiting Sonoma

Healdsburg is the natural base for Russian River, Dry Creek, and
Alexander Valley. Sonoma town anchors the southern part of the
county. Distances are larger than in Napa; many estates remain
appointment-only, but the small-town feel is less manicured than
across the Mayacamas.
