Washington is the United States' second-largest wine producer after
California, yet it remains under the radar of many drinkers. The
vineyards lie east of the Cascade Mountains in a high desert that
sees barely 20 centimetres of rain a year — irrigation is essential,
sunshine is abundant, and the long summer days followed by cold
nights give wines of ripe fruit and firm acidity. Cabernet
Sauvignon, Merlot, Syrah and Riesling all thrive here.

## 1. Quilceda Creek, Snohomish

The state's most revered Cabernet producer, founded in 1979.
Quilceda Creek's Columbia Valley Cabernet Sauvignon, sourced largely
from Champoux Vineyard and other top sites, has earned a long string
of near-perfect scores and is the benchmark for Washington's age-worthy
reds.

## 2. Leonetti Cellar, Walla Walla

The first commercial winery in Walla Walla, founded by Gary Figgins
in 1977. Leonetti's Cabernet Sauvignon and Reserve are cult wines
sold almost entirely through a mailing list. A family estate that
helped put the valley on the map.

## 3. Woodward Canyon, Lowden

Founded in 1981, the second winery in Walla Walla, known for
powerful, structured Cabernet — the Old Vines and the single-vineyard
bottlings — alongside one of the state's best Chardonnays. A pioneer
estate of great consistency.

## 4. Cayuse Vineyards, Walla Walla (The Rocks)

Christophe Baron's biodynamic estate, planted in the cobblestone
soils of the Rocks District of Milton-Freewater. Cayuse's Syrahs —
Bionic Frog, Cailloux, Armada — are intensely savoury, almost
Rhône-like, and among the most sought-after wines in America.

## 5. Château Ste. Michelle, Woodinville

The giant that built the modern industry, founded in its current
form in 1967. Its Riesling, made with input from Germany's Dr.
Loosen as the joint venture Eroica, and its Col Solare project with
Antinori on Red Mountain, anchor a vast and reliable portfolio.

## 6. Andrew Will, Vashon Island

Chris Camarda's estate, celebrated for Bordeaux-style blends named
after single vineyards — Sorella, Ciel du Cheval, Champoux. Elegant,
restrained reds that argue for terroir over power in Washington.

## 7. DeLille Cellars, Woodinville

Founded in 1992, a specialist in Bordeaux-style blends. Chaleur
Estate red and white, and the Syrah-based Doyenne wines, established
the polished, food-friendly style that defined Woodinville's tasting
scene.

## 8. L'Ecole No 41, Lowden

Housed in a historic 1915 schoolhouse, a Walla Walla pioneer founded
in 1983. Known for Merlot, Syrah and the Ferguson Vineyard estate
red on Walla Walla's slopes, plus a distinctive Sémillon.

## 9. Gramercy Cellars, Walla Walla

Master Sommelier Greg Harrington's estate, founded in 2005 and
focused on cool-climate Syrah and Cabernet in a restrained,
European-leaning style. A leader of Washington's newer, terroir-driven
generation.

## 10. Betz Family Winery, Redmond

Founded by Master of Wine Bob Betz, known for Rhône-inspired Syrahs
(La Côte Rousse, La Serenne) and Bordeaux blends sourced from the
state's top vineyards. Precise, balanced wines from a deeply
knowledgeable maker.

## Washington's appellations

The vast Columbia Valley AVA covers most of the state's vineyards and
contains the more specific sub-appellations that matter. Red
Mountain, the warmest and most tannic, is Washington's grand cru for
Cabernet. Walla Walla Valley, straddling the Oregon border, is the
fine-wine heart. The Rocks District of Milton-Freewater, with its
river-cobble soils, is the Syrah specialist. Horse Heaven Hills and
Yakima Valley supply much of the top fruit.

## Visiting Washington wine country

Two scenes dominate. Woodinville, just northeast of Seattle, packs
dozens of tasting rooms into a walkable district — convenient but far
from the vines. Walla Walla, a four-hour drive east, is the immersive
destination: a charming town surrounded by vineyards, with downtown
tasting rooms and estate visits among the wheat fields. Spring and
autumn are the ideal seasons.
