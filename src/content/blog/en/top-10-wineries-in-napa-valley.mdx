Napa Valley is California's most famous wine region — 50 km north of
San Francisco Bay, sheltered between the Mayacamas and Vaca ranges,
and devoted overwhelmingly to Cabernet Sauvignon. The 1976 Paris
Tasting put Napa on the map; these ten estates kept it there.

## 1. Robert Mondavi Winery, Oakville

The estate that built modern Napa. Founded in 1966, it pioneered
varietal labelling, French oak ageing, and serious estate marketing
in California.

## 2. Opus One, Oakville

The Mondavi–Rothschild joint venture from 1979, producing a single
Bordeaux-style blend from estate Oakville fruit. The architectural
showpiece of the To Kalon district.

## 3. Stag's Leap Wine Cellars, Stags Leap District

The estate whose Cabernet Sauvignon beat the first growths at the
1976 Paris Tasting. SLV and Cask 23 remain reference Napa Cabernets.

## 4. Screaming Eagle, Oakville

The pinnacle of Napa cult Cabernet. Tiny production, mailing-list
allocation, and prices among the highest for any New World wine.

## 5. Harlan Estate, Oakville

A hillside Oakville Cabernet of legendary status; Harlan Estate and
the second wine The Maiden are produced from a single property above
the valley.

## 6. Dominus Estate, Yountville

Christian Moueix's Napa estate (he is the Pétrus winemaker) — a
Bordeaux-style estate Cabernet from the Napanook vineyard.

## 7. Caymus Vineyards, Rutherford

A family-owned Rutherford estate whose ripe, oaky, immediately
appealing Cabernet shaped a whole school of Napa winemaking. The
Special Selection is the prestige bottling.

## 8. Beringer Vineyards, St. Helena

Napa's oldest continuously operating winery (1876). The Private
Reserve Cabernet is the prestige bottle; the historic Rhine House
mansion is open to the public.

## 9. Inglenook (formerly Niebaum-Coppola), Rutherford

The historic estate revived by Francis Ford Coppola; Rubicon is the
flagship Cabernet, and the chateau-style visitor experience is among
the best in the valley.

## 10. Schramsberg, Calistoga

The leading Napa sparkling wine house. Methode champenoise from
Chardonnay and Pinot Noir; the J. Schram tête de cuvée is the flagship.

## Visiting Napa Valley

Napa is the most visitor-organised wine region in the world. Highway
29 and the Silverado Trail run parallel through the valley with
hundreds of tasting rooms. Most estates require appointments; many
charge tasting fees that are waivered with purchase.
