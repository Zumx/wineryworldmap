The Rheingau is the spiritual home of German Riesling. A short bend
where the Rhine turns west forces it past a long, south-facing slope
of slate and quartzite on the Taunus foothills — and almost every
village on that ribbon has produced wine since the Cistercians of
Kloster Eberbach worked the vineyards in the 12th century. Today the
Rheingau is small (about 3,200 hectares) but historically and
stylistically central to any understanding of fine German wine.

## Schloss Johannisberg

The original Riesling estate. The first vineyard known to have been
planted exclusively with Riesling (1720s), and the documented source
of the first noble-rot Spätlese (1775, when a delayed harvest
courier turned a botrytised crop into the world's first late-harvest
wine by accident). Owned today by the Henkell-Freixenet group, still
making wines that taste of Rheingau slate.

## Robert Weil

Based in Kiedrich, founded 1875, now owned by the Suntory group but
run by Wilhelm Weil. The Kiedricher Gräfenberg is the standard-bearer
single vineyard. Weil's TBA bottlings, when conditions permit, are
among the world's most precise sweet wines.

## Schloss Vollrads

The estate at Schloss Vollrads has been documented since 1211 — one
of the oldest continuously operating wineries in the world. The
estate above Winkel has 80 hectares of Riesling and a stylistic
preference for medium-dry over fully dry expressions.

## Künstler

Gunter Künstler in Hochheim makes some of the most powerful dry
Rieslings of the Rheingau from the Hochheimer Hölle and Stielweg
sites. The Hölle is a steep south-facing parcel above the Main,
giving extraordinarily ripe but disciplined Riesling.

## Georg Breuer

Theresa Breuer leads the family estate in Rüdesheim. The Berg
Schlossberg and Berg Rottland are the great single vineyards;
Breuer's wines are dry-only and have a chiselled, mineral profile.

## Peter Jakob Kühn

Biodynamic pioneer in Oestrich. The Kühn family's wines are
extreme: low-yielding, long-fermenting, low-sulphur, and divisive in
the trade — but for Riesling drinkers who like texture and weight,
they are unmatched.

## Künstler (Hochheim)

Already mentioned among the Hölle specialists, Künstler also runs
several other Hochheim parcels including the Domdechaney, originally
the cathedral vineyards of the Mainz archbishops.

## Weingut Spreitzer

In Oestrich, the Spreitzer brothers (Andreas and Bernd) have built a
modern reputation for precise, dry Rieslings from the Lenchen and
Doosberg sites. Approachable wines without sacrificing the
Rheingau's intrinsic discipline.

## Domdechant Werner

Hochheim estate dating to 1780, owned by the Werner family for
generations. Holds prime parcels in the Hochheimer Domdechaney and
Kirchenstück — historically the source of Queen Victoria's
favoured "Hock", whence the English nickname for Rheingau wine.

## Eberbach Monastery

A working estate, not a museum. Kloster Eberbach (founded 1136 by the
Cistercians) still owns and farms the Steinberg, the largest
walled vineyard in Germany. The monastery hosts wine festivals and
is open year-round for visits and tastings.

## Visiting the Rheingau

The Rheinsteig hiking trail runs along the slope; small Vinotheks
operate in Rüdesheim, Hattenheim, Eltville, and Kiedrich. Most
estates open for casual visits on weekends. Find them on the
