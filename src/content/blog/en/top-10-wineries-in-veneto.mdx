Veneto produces more wine than any other Italian region, but its peaks
are concentrated in three places: Valpolicella for Amarone, Soave for
Garganega-based whites, and the Conegliano-Valdobbiadene hills for
Prosecco. These ten estates define the region's serious side.

## 1. Allegrini, Valpolicella

The most important quality producer in Valpolicella. Amarone with deep
concentration, La Poja (a varietal Corvina), and the single-vineyard La
Grola Veronese IGT are reference points.

## 2. Quintarelli, Valpolicella

The almost mythic Amarone producer — extreme ageing in old oak, tiny
production, and a long waiting list. Wines released only when the
winemaker considers them ready.

## 3. Bertani, Valpolicella

The historic Amarone house, with Amarone Classico labels dating to the
1950s still drinking well today. The traditional style benchmark.

## 4. Romano Dal Forno, Valpolicella

A modernist Amarone producer of extraordinary concentration. Vigneto
Monte Lodoletta Amarone is one of the most sought-after Italian reds.

## 5. Pieropan, Soave

The reference of Soave Classico — Calvarino and La Rocca are
single-vineyard Garganega from volcanic soil, capable of decades of
ageing.

## 6. Inama, Soave

A leading Soave Colli Berici estate; Vigneti di Foscarino and Vigneto
du Lot are top expressions of Garganega from volcanic terroir.

## 7. Anselmi, Soave (Veneto IGT)

The estate that left the Soave DOC to focus on quality outside the
appellation rules. Capitel Foscarino and Capitel Croce are reference
Garganega wines.

## 8. Bisol, Valdobbiadene

A leading Prosecco Superiore producer in the Cartizze hill — sparkling
Glera from the steepest UNESCO-listed vineyards of Conegliano-
Valdobbiadene.

## 9. Nino Franco, Valdobbiadene

A historic Prosecco house in the village of Valdobbiadene; Primo Franco
and Grave di Stecca are reference single-site Proseccos.

## 10. Tommasi, Valpolicella

A large, well-organised Valpolicella house with vineyards across the
Veneto and a strong visitor programme; Amarone Riserva Ca' Florian is
the prestige cuvée.

## Visiting Veneto

Verona is the natural base for Valpolicella and Soave (both within
thirty minutes by car); Treviso and Conegliano sit at the heart of
Prosecco country an hour east. Many Valpolicella estates run scheduled
tours; the Prosecco Hills (UNESCO-listed) reward a slow drive between
hilltop villages.
