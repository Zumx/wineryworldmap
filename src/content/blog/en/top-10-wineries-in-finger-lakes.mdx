The Finger Lakes lie in upstate New York, west of Syracuse and east
of Buffalo. The lakes are 11 long, deep, glacial bodies of water,
and Seneca and Cayuga (the two largest) are deep enough to act as
giant temperature buffers for the vineyards on their slopes —
without the lake effect, vines could not survive the upstate
winter. The region was the first in eastern North America to
commercially produce vinifera grapes (rather than American
hybrids), led by the Ukrainian-American viticulturist Dr. Konstantin
Frank in the 1950s. Today it is the most respected American region
for Riesling.

## Dr. Konstantin Frank

The founding estate. Konstantin Frank arrived in the US in 1952 and
proved that vinifera could survive Finger Lakes winters using
proper rootstocks. The estate, now run by his great-grandson Fred
Frank, produces Riesling, Rkatsiteli, and Pinot Noir from Keuka
Lake. Several bottlings are reference points for the region.

## Hermann J. Wiemer

Founded by the German-born winemaker of the same name. Now run by
Fred Merwarth and Oskar Bynke. The Magdalena Vineyard and HJW
single-vineyard Rieslings are the most highly regarded; the estate
also makes serious Cabernet Franc and traditional-method sparkling
wine.

## Ravines Wine Cellars

Founded by Morten and Lisa Hallgren. Their off-dry and dry
Rieslings have been particularly successful; the Argetsinger
Vineyard Riesling is the standard-bearer.

## Sheldrake Point

A Cayuga Lake estate notable for both still and ice wines. The
Wild Ferment Riesling and the Reserve Riesling are reliable
references; the late-harvest and ice-wine bottlings draw
international attention.

## Heart & Hands Wine Company

Tom and Susan Higgins's small Cayuga Lake estate, focused on Pinot
Noir and traditional-method sparkling. The Vintage Brut and
Estate Pinot Noir have built a strong critical reputation in the
last decade.

## Forge Cellars

A joint venture between Justin Boyette (American) and Louis Barruol
(of Château de Saint Cosme in the Rhône). The wines focus on
single-vineyard Riesling and Pinot Noir from Seneca Lake's steeper
slopes; the precision and minerality are notably Old-World in style.

## Red Newt Cellars

A long-established Seneca Lake estate. The Seneca Riesling and the
Tango Oaks Riesling are well-respected; the bistro at the winery
draws many visitors making the loop around Seneca Lake.

## Boundary Breaks

A Seneca Lake estate specialising in single-clone Rieslings — the
estate planted multiple Riesling clones in separate blocks so that
each could be bottled and labeled separately. The result is a fine
study in clonal variation in a single appellation.

## Bloomer Creek Vineyard

Kim Engle and Debra Bermingham's small Seneca Lake estate. The
wines are unconventional by Finger Lakes standards (extended
skin-contact whites, low-intervention winemaking) and have a strong
following among natural-wine drinkers.

## Lamoreaux Landing

A larger Seneca Lake estate with a long history. The estate
produces a full range of Riesling, Gewürztraminer, Pinot Noir, and
Cabernet Franc; the Estate Dry Riesling is a reliable benchmark.

## Visiting the Finger Lakes

Most visitors organise their trip around the Seneca Lake wine
trail or the Cayuga Lake wine trail; both have dozens of estates
within easy driving distance. Find them on the [interactive
