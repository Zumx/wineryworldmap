In the 1980s, planting wine grapes in England was an eccentric act.
Today it is, in places, a serious commercial proposition: the chalk
that runs from the Champagne region under the Channel and surfaces
again in the South Downs of Sussex and Kent has proven hospitable
to Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, and Pinot Meunier — the three classic
Champagne varieties. Sparkling wine made by the traditional method
is now England's calling card, and a handful of estates have
matured into genuine international quality.

## Nyetimber

The estate that launched modern English sparkling. American owners
Stuart and Sandy Moss planted Chardonnay and Pinots on West Sussex
chalk in 1988; the 1992 Classic Cuvée beat Champagne in a blind
tasting in 1996 and changed the trade's expectations of England.
Now under Eric Heerema's ownership, with multi-decade-aged Tillington
Single Vineyard the flagship.

## Gusbourne

Kent estate that built its reputation through stricter vintage
discipline than most Champagne houses — every Gusbourne is vintage-
dated. The Blanc de Blancs and the rare Fifty One Degrees North
prestige cuvée are among the most refined English sparkling wines
on the market.

## Ridgeview

Family-run by the Roberts family on the South Downs in Sussex.
Ridgeview's Bloomsbury, Cavendish, and Fitzrovia bottlings have for
twenty years been benchmarks for accessible, technically polished
English fizz. The estate was the first to win major international
sparkling-wine trophies.

## Hambledon Vineyard

Hampshire estate, claimed to be the longest commercially planted
vineyard in modern England (1952). Now under Bill Bordass's
ownership and Hervé Jestin's winemaking direction (formerly Duval-
Leroy in Champagne), producing Première Cuvée Classic and a
Premium Rosé.

## Wiston Estate

Sussex estate run by the Goring family. Dermot Sugrue's tenure as
winemaker (now departed for his own label) established Wiston's
single-vineyard, single-vintage bottlings. The Library releases age
beautifully and demonstrate genuine ageworthy potential.

## Sugrue South Downs

Dermot Sugrue's own label, established after his Wiston tenure.
Single-vineyard Pinot Noir and a flagship sparkling, The Trouble With
Dreams. Some of the most expressive small-volume English fizz.

## Camel Valley

Cornwall family estate run by the Lindo family. A different style —
slightly warmer, more aromatic — based on Bacchus, Pinot Noir,
and Seyval Blanc as well as the Champagne varieties. Bob Lindo
piloted the first commercial English wine to win at international
sparkling competition.

## Bolney Estate

Sussex estate now owned by Freixenet-Henkell. Bolney has produced
both still and sparkling wines for nearly forty years, with the
Eighteen Acre Rosé sparkling and the Lychgate Bacchus still white
as benchmarks for accessible English wine.

## Chapel Down

Kent estate that has scaled to become England's largest commercial
wine producer. Chapel Down's Tenterden visitor centre and broad
distribution have made the brand the most internationally visible
English wine, while the Kit's Coty single-vineyard line shows the
estate's high-end ambition.

## Hush Heath Estate

Kent estate founded by Richard Balfour-Lynn. The Balfour Brut Rosé
established a reputation for fresh, light English sparkling rosé,
and the estate has developed a strong wine-and-food destination
operation alongside the production cellar.

## Visiting English Wine Country

Sussex and Kent concentrate most of the major estates and are an
hour or two from London by train. Many estates offer tours on
weekends and through summer; cellar-door direct sales are increasingly
common. The South Downs Way passes within hiking distance of
several Sussex producers. Find every estate on the [interactive
