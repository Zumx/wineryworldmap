The Wachau is a narrow stretch of the Danube between Melk and
Krems, in Lower Austria, where vines climb terraces of gneiss and
loess and almost every house in the riverside villages of Spitz,
Weissenkirchen, Dürnstein, and Loiben belongs to or supplies a
winery. Two grape varieties dominate: Grüner Veltliner, the
white-pepper-and-green-apple variety that is Austria's signature
grape, and Riesling, here grown on terraces that are some of the
steepest worked vineyards in the world. The Vinea Wachau growers'
association uses three quality categories — Steinfeder, Federspiel,
and Smaragd — to describe ripeness, and the Smaragd wines are the
region's flagships.

## F. X. Pichler

Lucas Pichler now runs the estate founded by his father, Franz
Xaver. The Loibenberg and Kellerberg single vineyards produce some
of the most concentrated and long-lived dry Rieslings and Grüner
Veltliners in Austria.

## Emmerich Knoll

A Loiben estate famed for its Baroque-style label depicting Saint
Urban. The Knoll family's wines — particularly the Loibenberg
Riesling Smaragd and the Schütt Grüner Veltliner Smaragd — are
benchmarks for the long-lived, mineral Wachau style.

## Franz Hirtzberger

Spitz-based estate run by Franz Hirtzberger junior. The Singerriedel
Riesling is the standard-bearer for the village of Spitz and one of
the most aromatically precise Rieslings in Austria.

## Rudi Pichler

A modern Wachau estate (no relation to F. X. Pichler), known for
particularly precise, mineral wines from the Hochrain and Kollmütz
vineyards. The estate is biodynamic.

## Domäne Wachau

The largest cooperative-style producer in the Wachau, with 250
member growers across all the great vineyards. The Achleiten,
Kellerberg, and Singerriedel single-vineyard bottlings, made from
the best parcels of the cooperative's members, demonstrate how high
a co-op model can reach.

## Prager

A Weissenkirchen estate now run by Toni Bodenstein, whose
single-vineyard Wachstum Bodenstein Grüner Veltliner is one of the
most interesting clonal-selection projects in Austrian wine.

## Tegernseerhof

A small Dürnstein estate known for precise, transparent, and
relatively early-drinking Grüner Veltliner and Riesling. The
Bergdistel and Frauenweingärten bottlings offer outstanding value.

## Alzinger

Leo Alzinger is one of the most respected Wachau producers despite
keeping a low public profile. The Loibenberg and Steinertal Smaragd
bottlings are the standard-bearers; minimal-intervention, terroir-driven
wines.

## Nikolaihof

The oldest winery in Austria, with documented winemaking history
going back to Roman times. Nikolaihof has been biodynamic since
1971 and is widely cited as Europe's first certified biodynamic
estate. The Vinothek Riesling, aged for 17 years before release,
is a famously unusual wine.

## Hirsch (Heiligenstein)

The Hirsch estate is technically just outside the Wachau in the
Kamptal, but is included in many Wachau-adjacent tasting tours.
The Heiligenstein Riesling from old vines on red sandstone is one
of Austria's greatest Rieslings.

## Visiting the Wachau

The villages along the Danube are small and walkable. Krems and
Melk anchor the two ends; Dürnstein, with its blue church and the
ruined castle where Richard the Lionheart was held, makes a
striking centre. Most estates open by appointment. Find them on the
[interactive map](/map) — filter to the Wachau.
