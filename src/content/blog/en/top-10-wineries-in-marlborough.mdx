Marlborough at the top of New Zealand's South Island is the world's
most famous Sauvignon Blanc region. The Wairau Valley produces the
classic herbaceous-tropical style; the cooler Awatere Valley adds
saline tension. Beyond Sauvignon, Marlborough also turns out
exceptional Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and traditional-method sparkling.

## 1. Cloudy Bay, Wairau Valley

The estate that put New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc on the world map in
the 1980s. Te Koko (oak-aged Sauvignon) and Pelorus (sparkling) are
the prestige expressions.

## 2. Dog Point, Wairau Valley

Founded by former Cloudy Bay viticulturalists; Section 94 (oak-aged
Sauvignon) and the single-vineyard Pinot Noirs are reference Marlborough.

## 3. Greywacke, Wairau Valley

Kevin Judd's estate (also ex-Cloudy Bay); wild-fermented Sauvignon
Blanc, fine Pinot Noir, and a small but respected portfolio.

## 4. Te Whare Ra, Wairau Valley

A small biodynamic Marlborough estate making one of the country's
best Sauvignon Blancs alongside Gewurztraminer, Riesling, and Pinot
Noir from old vines.

## 5. Seresin, Wairau Valley

A biodynamic estate founded by cinematographer Michael Seresin;
Marama (oak-aged Sauvignon) and the Sun & Moon Pinot Noir are the
flagships.

## 6. Villa Maria, Auckland-Marlborough

New Zealand's largest family-owned wine company. The Reserve and
Single Vineyard Marlborough Sauvignon Blancs are reference wines and
the visitor experience in Auckland is excellent.

## 7. Felton Road's neighbour — actually let's say Yealands, Awatere Valley

A large sustainable Awatere Valley estate with a strong visitor
programme; the cooler Awatere Sauvignon Blanc is the flagship.

## 8. Saint Clair, Marlborough

A leading Marlborough single-vineyard specialist; the Pioneer Block
series maps the appellation through individually-bottled Sauvignon
Blancs from different sites.

## 9. Brancott Estate, Wairau Valley

The estate that pioneered Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc (planted in
1973). The Letter Series wines and the original Brancott label
remain reference points.

## 10. Hunter's Wines, Wairau Valley

A long-established family estate with strong single-vineyard
Sauvignon Blanc and a pioneering role in Marlborough sparkling.

## Visiting Marlborough

Blenheim is the natural base, a 25-minute flight from Wellington. The
Wairau Valley concentrates the historic estates within easy biking
distance; the Awatere Valley is further south and best driven. Many
estates have restaurants on site and the region is set up for casual,
appointment-free visits.
