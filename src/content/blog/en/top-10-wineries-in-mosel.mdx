The Mosel is a winding ribbon of slate vineyards in western Germany. Its
Rieslings — featherlight, slate-driven, and astonishingly age-worthy — are
made on slopes so steep they still require hand-work and monorack
winches. The ten estates below are the names that shape the region.

## 1. Egon Müller, Wiltingen (Scharzhof)

The single most famous Riesling estate in the world. The Scharzhofberg
Auslese, Beerenauslese, and Trockenbeerenauslese are sold at the
Trier auction at record-setting prices.

## 2. Joh. Jos. Prüm, Wehlen

The Wehlener Sonnenuhr Auslese is one of the most recognisable Rieslings
on Earth — fruity, slate-driven, built to age fifty years. Family-run,
classical in style.

## 3. Dr. Loosen, Bernkastel

A leading mid-Mosel estate with holdings in all the great vineyards —
Erdener Prälat, Treppchen, Würzgarten, Wehlener Sonnenuhr — and a
welcoming visitor programme in Bernkastel.

## 4. Selbach-Oster, Zeltingen

A family estate with Sonnenuhr and Schlossberg holdings, known for
extremely precise dry and feinherb (off-dry) Rieslings from old vines.

## 5. Markus Molitor, Wehlen

A major mid-Mosel estate with vineyards across Wehlen, Zeltingen, and
the Saar; the white-capsule Auslese trocken bottlings are reference dry
Rieslings.

## 6. Fritz Haag, Brauneberg

The reference estate of Brauneberger Juffer-Sonnenuhr — fine,
filigreed Rieslings from a south-facing slate amphitheatre on the
mid-Mosel.

## 7. Willi Schaefer, Graach

A tiny family estate with holdings in Graacher Domprobst and
Himmelreich; some of the most age-worthy and tightly-wound Rieslings on
the Mosel.

## 8. Van Volxem, Wiltingen

The leading modern Saar estate, focused on dry Riesling from steep
slate sites; restored prestige to the Saar after decades of decline.

## 9. Weingut Karthäuserhof, Eitelsbach

The historic Ruwer estate inside the Karthäuserhofberg monopole;
slate-driven Rieslings of cool restraint and long life.

## 10. Forstmeister Geltz Zilliken, Saarburg

A Saar estate with Saarburger Rausch holdings — known for some of the
finest sweet Rieslings produced today, with deep cellar tradition.

## How Mosel visits work

The Mosel is set up for visitors more than any other German wine region.
Bernkastel-Kues and Traben-Trarbach are ideal bases, with riverside
hotels and tasting rooms within walking distance. Larger estates take
appointments; smaller ones may welcome casual visitors at posted hours.
