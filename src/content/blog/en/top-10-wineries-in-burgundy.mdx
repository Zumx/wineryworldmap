Burgundy is a region of villages, walls, and tiny parcels. Two grape
varieties — Pinot Noir and Chardonnay — and several thousand named *climats*
make this the world's most fragmented great wine region. The ten domaines
below are reference points; most of them do not accept casual visitors.

## 1. Domaine de la Romanée-Conti, Vosne-Romanée

The mythic estate. A handful of grand-cru monopoles produce some of the
rarest and most expensive wines on Earth. There is no public visit.

## 2. Domaine Leroy, Vosne-Romanée

Lalou Bize-Leroy's uncompromising biodynamic estate, with grand-cru holdings
across the Côte de Nuits and Côte de Beaune.

## 3. Domaine Leflaive, Puligny-Montrachet

The benchmark for white Burgundy and an early adopter of biodynamics in the
Côte de Beaune.

## 4. Domaine Armand Rousseau, Gevrey-Chambertin

The reference for Chambertin and Chambertin-Clos de Bèze — long-aged,
perfumed reds from the heart of the Côte de Nuits.

## 5. Domaine Comte Georges de Vogüé, Chambolle-Musigny

Custodian of the lion's share of Musigny grand cru and Bonnes-Mares; wines
of perfumed, silky power.

## 6. Domaine Coche-Dury, Meursault

The reference for Meursault and a tiny parcel of Corton-Charlemagne;
practically uncontactable for visits.

## 7. Domaine Méo-Camuzet, Vosne-Romanée

A modern Vosne domaine with holdings in Richebourg, Clos de Vougeot, and the
Vosne premiers crus, plus an accessible négociant line.

## 8. Domaine Dujac, Morey-Saint-Denis

Whole-cluster Pinot Noir of perfume and finesse from Clos Saint-Denis,
Clos de la Roche, and the Vosne grands crus.

## 9. Maison Louis Jadot, Beaune

A leading négociant-éleveur with significant domaine holdings; one of the
few major Burgundy names with a structured visitor programme in Beaune.

## 10. Maison Joseph Drouhin, Beaune

Another historic négociant-éleveur in the centre of Beaune, with deep
cellars under the town and an Oregon offshoot that helped define new-world
Pinot.

## How Burgundy visits actually work

Almost no top grower receives the public. Realistic access is through
Beaune négociant tasting rooms, village *caveaux*, hospice tours, and the
many excellent smaller estates within the named villages. A local guide
who has standing appointments is the single best investment.
