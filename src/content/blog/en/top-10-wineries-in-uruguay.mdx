Uruguay is South America's fourth-largest wine producer and far
the most maritime. The country sits between the Rio de la Plata
and the Atlantic, with no point more than 200 km from saltwater,
and the wines reflect that proximity: fresh, structured, often
saline reds, distinct from the more powerful Argentine and Chilean
profiles. The signature grape is Tannat — brought from Madiran in
southwest France by Basque immigrants in 1870 — and Uruguay now
produces more fine Tannat than any other country.

## Bodega Garzón

Founded by Argentine billionaire Alejandro Bulgheroni in the
Maldonado coastal region. The estate is one of the largest
sustainability-certified wineries in the world (LEED Platinum) and
produces Albariño, Tannat, and Cabernet Franc that have collected
international awards. The restaurant is on every Maldonado wine
itinerary.

## Bouza

A Montevideo-based estate, family-owned, with vineyards in
Canelones, Las Violetas, and the Pan de Azúcar hills. Bouza was the
first Uruguayan producer to commercially bottle Albariño; the
Tannat and Tempranillo are also benchmarks of the modern Uruguayan
style.

## Pisano

A long-established family estate in Canelones, now run by the
third generation. The Río Grande Tannat and the Axis Mundi Tannat
are flagships; Pisano also makes well-regarded Petit Verdot and
sparkling wines.

## Familia Deicas

A larger Canelones estate producing reds across multiple ranges.
The Atlántico Sur Tannat and the Massimo Deicas Tannat are at the
top of the range; the entry-level Don Próspero is one of the most
exported Uruguayan reds.

## Juanicó (Bodegas Familia Deicas)

Closely associated with Familia Deicas. The Juanicó label
includes the well-known Don Pascual range, distributed
internationally and a useful entry point to Uruguayan wine.

## Vinos Finos H. Stagnari

A historic family estate, founded by Hector Stagnari in 1928 and
known for some of Uruguay's most age-worthy Tannat. The Dayman
Vineyard Tannat and the Stagnari Reserva Tannat are reference
bottlings.

## Establecimiento Juanicó

(See Juanicó above.) The estate has historically been one of the
largest Uruguayan exporters and a force in establishing Tannat
internationally.

## Bodega Cerro Chapeu

A Bordeaux-style estate in the Rivera region in northern Uruguay,
near the Brazilian border. The Cerro Chapeu Tannat and Cabernet
Sauvignon show that fine wine is possible well away from the
Atlantic coast.

## Marichal

A Canelones family estate with a long tradition of Tannat
production. The Reserve Collection and Premium Tannat have built a
solid international reputation.

## Pizzorno Family Estate

Founded by an Italian immigrant in 1910, now in the fourth
generation. The Reserva Tannat and the Don Próspero (no relation to
Familia Deicas's Don Próspero) are reliable standards; the estate
also experiments with biodynamic farming in selected parcels.

## Visiting Uruguay

Most estates are within 90 minutes' drive of Montevideo, in
Canelones, Las Violetas, and Carrasco. The Maldonado coast,
including Garzón, is two hours east. Find them on the [interactive
