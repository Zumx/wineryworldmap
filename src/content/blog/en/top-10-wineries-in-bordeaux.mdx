Bordeaux is the world's largest fine-wine region and the historical reference
point for Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Sauvignon Blanc-Sémillon blends.
The classification of 1855 still shapes prices, perceptions, and which gates
will open to a polite request.

## 1. Château Margaux, Margaux

A first growth of 1855 housed in a neo-Palladian château at the heart of the
Médoc. The grand vin is the model of perfumed, ethereal Cabernet.

## 2. Château Lafite Rothschild, Pauillac

The most coveted first growth, with deep, slow-evolving wines that have
become a benchmark of investment-grade Bordeaux.

## 3. Château Mouton Rothschild, Pauillac

Promoted to first growth in 1973 and famous for its artist-designed labels
and the on-site museum of wine art.

## 4. Château Latour, Pauillac

The most muscular of the first growths, built for decades of cellar age and
recognisable by the iconic tower on its label.

## 5. Château Haut-Brion, Pessac-Léognan

The only non-Médoc first growth, in the Bordeaux suburbs, producing both a
legendary red and one of the world's great dry whites.

## 6. Château Pétrus, Pomerol

The Right Bank's most legendary estate — a tiny clay-rich plot of nearly pure
Merlot, with no formal classification and prices that need none.

## 7. Château Cheval Blanc, Saint-Émilion

A Premier Grand Cru Classé A famous for an unusual Cabernet Franc-led blend
and a striking Christian de Portzamparc cellar.

## 8. Château Ausone, Saint-Émilion

The other historical Saint-Émilion summit — a tiny hillside estate on
limestone with extraordinary longevity.

## 9. Château d'Yquem, Sauternes

The pinnacle of botrytised sweet wine; a single hilltop estate above the
Garonne producing the most age-worthy dessert wine in the world.

## 10. Château Pichon Comtesse, Pauillac

A super-second growth often outperforming first growths in blind tastings,
with one of the most welcoming visitor programmes among the Médoc classed
growths.

## How Bordeaux visits actually work

Most classed growths receive only by appointment and many only through the
trade. The négociant system means casual cellar-door sales are rare; the
city's Cité du Vin and a handful of open-door châteaux on the D2 are the
realistic starting points.
