For most of the 20th century, Sicily exported bulk wine in tanker
trucks. Since the 1990s, a small group of growers has turned the
island into one of Italy's most exciting fine-wine regions. Two
centres drive the renaissance: Mount Etna in the east, where black
volcanic soils and old-vine Nerello Mascalese produce wines often
compared to Burgundy; and the western coast, where Nero d'Avola,
Frappato, and Carricante express a sun-drenched but increasingly
elegant character.

## Tasca d'Almerita

The grand old name of Sicilian wine. The Regaleali estate in the
island's interior pioneered estate bottling in Sicily in the 1950s,
and the family now operates additional estates on Etna, Salina,
Mozia, and Capofaro. The Rosso del Conte is the flagship.

## Planeta

Founded by the Planeta family in the 1990s, with vineyards across
five Sicilian zones (Menfi, Vittoria, Etna, Noto, and Capo
Milazzo). Planeta's modern style brought Sicilian wine onto export
markets internationally. The Cometa Fiano is a benchmark Sicilian
white.

## Donnafugata

A Marsala-based estate that has built a reputation for Nero d'Avola
and innovative blends. The Mille e Una Notte is the prestige
cuvée; the Sedàra is a reliable everyday Nero d'Avola.

## Benanti

Pioneer of the Etna renaissance. The family planted Nerello
Mascalese at high altitude on the volcano's north slope and
demonstrated that ancient pre-phylloxera vines could produce
world-class wine. The Pietra Marina (Carricante) is one of Italy's
greatest whites.

## Passopisciaro

Andrea Franchetti's Etna estate, founded in 2000. The single-contrada
bottlings (Porcaria, Chiappemacine, Sciaranuova, Rampante,
Guardiola) made the contrada concept famous and shaped how the
appellation now describes itself.

## Frank Cornelissen

Belgian-born Frank Cornelissen settled on the northern slope of
Etna in 2001 to make natural wines from old-vine Nerello Mascalese
on terraced lava. The Magma is one of the most sought-after
natural wines in Europe; the MunJebel is more accessible.

## Tenuta delle Terre Nere

Marc de Grazia, an Italian-American wine importer, founded the
estate on Etna's north slope in 2002. The single-contrada wines
were among the first to be exported widely. The Calderara
Sottana is the standard-bearer.

## Cos

A Vittoria estate co-founded by Giambattista Cilia, Cirino Strano,
and Giusto Occhipinti in 1980. The Cerasuolo di Vittoria (a blend
of Nero d'Avola and Frappato) is one of Sicily's most distinctive
appellations, and Cos's amphora-aged versions are among the most
admired natural wines on the island.

## Arianna Occhipinti

Arianna Occhipinti, niece of Cos co-founder Giusto Occhipinti,
founded her own estate near Vittoria in 2004. The Frappato is one
of Sicily's most beloved expressions of the variety; the SP68
(Frappato-Nero d'Avola) has built a global cult.

## Graci

Alberto Graci's Etna estate, on the north slope, makes
single-contrada wines (Arcurìa, Feudo di Mezzo, Barbabecchi) that
demonstrate the precision and elegance of high-altitude Nerello
Mascalese. The Quota 600 and Quota 1000 reds explore the altitude
dimension explicitly.

## Visiting Sicily

The island is large and the wine regions distant from one another;
Etna alone deserves several days. Estates in Catania, Ragusa,
Trapani, and Agrigento provinces all welcome visitors with prior
booking. Find them on the [interactive map](/map) — filter to
Sicily.
