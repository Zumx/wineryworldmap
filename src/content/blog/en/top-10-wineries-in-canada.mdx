Canada's wine identity has been shaped by two distinct regions separated by the Rocky Mountains: Ontario's Niagara Peninsula — famous above all for ice wine — and British Columbia's Okanagan Valley, a desert landscape of dramatic lake-country scenery and rapidly improving red and white wine production. A third emerging region, Nova Scotia, produces sparkling wine and Tidal Bay white blends from L'Acadie Blanc and other cool-climate varieties. Together they represent a wine culture still young by global standards but already producing wines that compete at the highest international levels.

## 1. Inniskillin, Niagara Peninsula

Inniskillin is the estate that put Canadian wine on the world map. Founded in 1975 by Karl Kaiser and Donald Ziraldo — among the first winery licences issued in Ontario since Prohibition — it became internationally famous when its 1989 Vidal Icewine won a Grand Prix d'Honneur at Vinexpo Bordeaux in 1991. Canadian Vidal Icewine, harvested naturally frozen at -8°C or below (temperatures reliably achieved most years in Niagara), has a mandarin orange and tropical fruit sweetness balanced by crisp acidity. Inniskillin's Riesling Icewine and Oak Aged Vidal Icewine are the prestige expressions alongside the entry-level Vidal. The estate also produces table wines — Montague Estate Pinot Noir and Brae Burn Estate Chardonnay — from a cooler site in the Niagara Escarpment. The winery barn, dating to 1920, houses the cellar door and is one of the most visited wine destinations in Canada.

## 2. Mission Hill Family Estate, Okanagan Valley

Anthony von Mandl's Mission Hill, perched dramatically above Okanagan Lake in West Kelowna, is the most architecturally impressive winery in Canada. The bell tower, amphitheatre, and Antinori-influenced design by Tom Kundig give Mission Hill the feel of a European estate transplanted to desert hill country. The flagship Oculus (Merlot-dominant Bordeaux blend with Cabernet Sauvignon, Cabernet Franc, and Malbec) is consistently one of the finest reds produced in BC; the Quatrain and Perpetua Chardonnay round out the reserve range. Mission Hill offers extensive visitor experiences including open-air concerts in the vineyard amphitheatre and multiple tasting options across its several visitor venues.

## 3. CedarCreek Estate Winery, Okanagan Valley

CedarCreek, also in the Kelowna area of the Okanagan, has undergone a significant transformation since the Bronfman family's Mark Anthony Group (the company behind White Claw hard seltzer) acquired it in 2014. Under winemaker Markus Fahrer, the estate has shifted decisively toward Burgundian varieties. The Platinum Block 2 Pinot Noir and Platinum Block 7 Chardonnay are single-vineyard prestige expressions from the home estate and rank among the finest Burgundian-variety wines in Canada. The estate's Home Block Pinot Noir is the most accessible premium expression, while the Platinum tier consistently shows that the Okanagan's cooler benches can produce genuinely Burgundian-scaled wine.

## 4. Tinhorn Creek Vineyards, South Okanagan

Tinhorn Creek in Oliver, in the warmer South Okanagan subregion, is one of the most reliable producers of Cabernet Franc, Merlot, and Cabernet Sauvignon from the semi-arid conditions suited to Bordeaux varieties. The Oldfield Series Cabernet Franc is the flagship, showing the variety's characteristic bell pepper, dark cherry, and graphite character in a distinctive South Okanagan frame. The Winemaker's Red Blend is the prestige multi-varietal expression. Tinhorn Creek is also known for amphitheatre concerts and one of the most accessible visitor experiences in the Oliver-Osoyoos area, often called Canada's wine capital.

## 5. Burrowing Owl Estate Winery, South Okanagan

Named for the endangered burrowing owl (a conservation programme is embedded in the estate's operations), Burrowing Owl in Oliver produces Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, and Pinot Gris from 150 acres of Black Sage Bench vineyard. The Athene (a Syrah-dominant blend) is the prestige label; the Cabernet Franc and Syrah are consistently among the best varietal expressions in BC. The estate inn — a Mediterranean-styled boutique accommodation — and rooftop patio restaurant make Burrowing Owl one of the few winery destinations in Canada where you can eat, sleep, and taste without leaving the property.

## 6. Le Vieux Pin, South Okanagan

Le Vieux Pin (The Old Pine), based in Oliver on the Black Sage Bench, is one of the most ambitious small producers in British Columbia. The focus is on Rhône and Burgundy varieties: Equinoxe Syrah, Avarice Merlot, and Époque are the prestige labels. Winemaker Severine Pinte applies French training to South Okanagan fruit with results that consistently challenge the dominance of larger BC producers. The Vaïla Pinot Gris — whole-cluster pressed, with extended lees contact — is one of the most distinctive whites produced in the province.

## 7. Nk'Mip Cellars, South Okanagan

Nk'Mip (pronounced "ink-a-meep") Cellars in Osoyoos is North America's first Indigenous-owned and operated winery, established in 2002 by the Osoyoos Indian Band on land overlooking Osoyoos Lake. The estate produces Syrah, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Riesling from the warmest, driest corner of British Columbia — the only true desert in Canada. The Winemaker's Nest series (Merlot, Syrah, Chardonnay) represents the flagship tier; the Mer'r'iym Bordeaux blend is produced only in exceptional years. The Desert Cultural Centre adjacent to the winery connects the wine experience to the history and culture of the Syilx/Okanagan Nation.

## 8. Quails' Gate Estate Winery, Okanagan Valley

Quails' Gate, situated on the slopes above Okanagan Lake in West Kelowna, is the most decorated producer of Pinot Noir and Chardonnay in British Columbia. The Stewart Family Reserve Pinot Noir and the Très Vieilles Vignes Chenin Blanc (from the oldest Chenin Blanc vines in BC, planted 1961) are the prestige expressions. The Old Vines Foch — made from the hybrid Marechal Foch variety planted by the Stewart family in the 1960s — is a deliberately cultivated curiosity, one of the more distinctive wines produced in Canada. The estate restaurant, Quails' Gate Restaurant, is among the most-awarded winery restaurants in the country.

## 9. Tantalus Vineyards, Okanagan Valley

Tantalus, in East Kelowna on the Naramata-adjacent bench, is best known for producing some of Canada's finest Riesling from a 1978-planted parcel — old vines by BC standards. The Old Vines Riesling is the prestige expression: bone dry, mineral, and capable of aging for fifteen or more years, it demonstrates that the Okanagan can produce Riesling at a Mosel or Alsace level of interest. The estate also produces Pinot Noir and Chardonnay of genuine quality. The recently redesigned winery building, opened in 2010, is among the most architecturally coherent in BC.

## 10. Painted Rock Estate Winery, South Okanagan

Painted Rock in Penticton's Skaha Bench produces Bordeaux varieties and Syrah from a single south-facing vineyard overlooking Skaha Lake. The Red Icon (Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Cabernet Franc, Malbec, Petit Verdot) is the prestige Bordeaux blend and one of the most discussed red wines in BC. The estate is small by Okanagan standards, focused entirely on premium production, and the combination of John Skinner's commitment to quality and the site's exceptional sun exposure has produced a run of vintages since 2007 that have established Painted Rock as a cult producer within Canada.

## Niagara vs Okanagan vs Nova Scotia

The three regions produce fundamentally different wine. Niagara Peninsula, with its continental climate moderated by Lake Ontario, excels in Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, Riesling, Cabernet Franc, and above all ice wine — the extreme cold of Niagara winters is the region's defining asset. Okanagan Valley, a semi-arid desert running north-south between mountains, produces richer, riper reds in the South Okanagan and more elegant cool-climate whites and Pinot Noir on the cooler Northern benches. Nova Scotia's Annapolis Valley and Malagash Peninsula produce sparkling wine, Tidal Bay blends, and aromatic whites from hybrids and cool-climate European varieties, in a style reminiscent of Northern France.
