Tuscany is the home of Sangiovese, and its three great expressions —
Chianti Classico, Brunello di Montalcino, and the Super Tuscans of
Bolgheri — supply the canon of modern Italian fine wine. These ten
estates are the names that move markets.

## 1. Antinori, San Casciano

The Antinori family has made wine since 1385. Tignanello (Sangiovese-
Cabernet) and Solaia, both born here in the 1970s, kicked off the Super
Tuscan movement and changed Italian wine forever.

## 2. Marchesi de' Frescobaldi, Florence

A thousand-year wine family with ten Tuscan estates — Castello di
Nipozzano in Chianti Rufina, Castelgiocondo in Montalcino, joint
ownership of Ornellaia and Masseto in Bolgheri.

## 3. Biondi-Santi, Montalcino

The founding estate of Brunello di Montalcino. Ferruccio Biondi-Santi
isolated the Brunello clone of Sangiovese in the 19th century; the Il
Greppo estate is the historical heart of the appellation.

## 4. Tenuta San Guido, Bolgheri

The home of Sassicaia — the wine that created the Bolgheri DOC and
remains its only single-property denomination. Cabernet on the Tuscan
coast, with a Bordelais reference frame.

## 5. Ornellaia, Bolgheri

The other great Bolgheri estate, now Frescobaldi-owned. The Ornellaia
Cabernet-Merlot blend and the separate Masseto Merlot are reference
points of Italian fine wine.

## 6. Castello di Ama, Gaiole in Chianti

A Chianti Classico estate that helped lead the region's modern
revival — single-vineyard Sangiovese (Vigneto Bellavista, La Casuccia)
and a stunning contemporary art programme on site.

## 7. Castello dei Rampolla, Panzano

A leading Chianti Classico estate known for biodynamic farming and the
Sammarco (Cabernet Sauvignon) Super Tuscan.

## 8. Fontodi, Panzano

An organic Chianti Classico estate in the Conca d'Oro of Panzano;
Vigna del Sorbo and Flaccianello della Pieve are reference Sangioveses.

## 9. Soldera Case Basse, Montalcino

A cult Brunello producer (now bottled as Toscana IGT after a long
dispute) — extreme purity, long ageing, tiny production, and prices to
match.

## 10. Castello Banfi, Montalcino

One of the largest Brunello estates and an excellent visitor experience
— a restored medieval castle, hospitality programme, restaurant, and
wine museum on the property.

## Visiting Tuscany

Tuscany is set up for the wine traveller. Florence and Siena bracket
Chianti Classico; Montalcino sits south on a hilltop; Bolgheri is on
the coast west of Grosseto. Most estates accept appointments, and many
run scheduled visitor programmes with restaurants and overnight stays.
