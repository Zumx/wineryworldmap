Penfolds Grange is the wine that established Australia as a
producer of fine wine outside the European framework. First made
in 1951 by Penfolds chief winemaker Max Schubert, after a study
trip to Bordeaux, Grange is a powerful, Shiraz-dominated red, aged
in American oak, made from grapes sourced across several South
Australian regions — most notably Barossa Valley, McLaren Vale,
and Coonawarra. It is a deliberately multi-regional wine, a fact
that has informed Penfolds's blending philosophy for seventy
years.

## The origins

Max Schubert visited Bordeaux in 1950 and was inspired by the
ageing potential of First Growth Cabernet Sauvignon. Returning to
Australia, he set out to make a wine that could age comparably,
choosing Shiraz (which thrived in South Australia in a way
Cabernet did not) rather than Cabernet for the base. The first
vintages were controversial within Penfolds; the board ordered
Schubert to stop in 1957, but he made the 1957, 1958, and 1959
vintages secretly. By the early 1960s the wines from 1951-1955
had matured and Grange was recognised as a wine of singular
quality.

## The blending philosophy

Grange has always been blended across multiple South Australian
regions and parcels. The exact composition varies each vintage:
Barossa Valley typically provides the majority share, McLaren
Vale supplies depth and structure, Coonawarra adds occasional
Cabernet (small percentages — Grange is now sometimes 100%
Shiraz, sometimes 95% Shiraz with 5% Cabernet Sauvignon depending
on the year). The blending is done after fermentation, in batches,
with the chief winemaker drawing on hundreds of parcels.

## Vinification

Fermentation takes place in stainless steel; the wine is then
moved to American oak barriques (50% new) for 18 to 20 months of
ageing. The American oak is what gives Grange its distinctive
profile — sweet vanilla, dill, coconut, dark chocolate — that
distinguishes it from European-style Shiraz aged in French oak.

## The cellaring tradition

Grange is built to age. The current chief winemaker recommends a
minimum of 12 years' cellaring before opening; older vintages can
develop for 50 years or more. The 1955, 1971, 1976, 1986, 1990,
1996, 2002, 2006, and 2010 vintages are particular benchmarks.

## The 2008 vintage

The 2008 vintage of Grange received perfect 100-point scores from
both Wine Spectator and James Halliday — an extraordinarily rare
double — and remains the highest-rated Grange to date. It marked
the moment when the international wine establishment fully
embraced Grange as a peer to top Bordeaux.

## Penfolds beyond Grange

Penfolds also produces several other red wines of exceptional
quality — Bin 707 Cabernet Sauvignon, Bin 389 Cabernet Shiraz
(known as "Baby Grange"), and the limited-release Bin 95 Saint
Henri Shiraz. The Bin system has been in use since 1959 and the
numbered wines have a structured pricing hierarchy.

## Visiting Penfolds

Penfolds operates the Magill Estate in suburban Adelaide (the
historic founding site) and the Barossa Valley winery, both open
to visitors. Grange itself is not produced at any single estate —
the multi-regional blend is assembled at Penfolds's central
winemaking facilities.
