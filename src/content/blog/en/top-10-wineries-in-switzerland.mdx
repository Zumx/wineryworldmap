Switzerland is one of Europe's most fascinating wine countries, and
one of the least familiar to outsiders — because the Swiss drink
almost all their own wine, and only one or two percent of
production is exported. The vineyards climb the Alpine and
sub-Alpine slopes of six recognised regions: Valais, Vaud, Geneva,
Three Lakes, Ticino, and Eastern Switzerland. The signature varieties
include Chasselas (the regional white grape, light and saline),
Petite Arvine and Heida from Valais, Pinot Noir across the
country, and Merlot in Ticino. To drink the best Swiss wines you
have to visit.

## Marie-Thérèse Chappaz

The patriarch of Valais biodynamic wine. Chappaz's small estate
near Fully produces some of Switzerland's most respected wines,
including extraordinary Petite Arvine, Marsanne (locally called
Ermitage), and late-harvest sweet wines.

## Domaine Jean-René Germanier

A larger Valais estate with significant land in Vétroz. The Cayas
Syrah and the Amigne de Vétroz Mitis (an off-dry expression of the
local Amigne grape) are particular reference points.

## Pierre-Maurice Carruzzo

A Vétroz estate also focused on the local grape Amigne, which is
nearly extinct outside this single village. Carruzzo's dry Amigne
is one of the most distinctive Swiss whites.

## Domaine Henri Cruchon

A Vaud estate in Échichens, biodynamic. The Chasselas bottlings
from different parcels around Lake Geneva show the regional
nuance; the estate also makes well-respected Pinot Noir and
Gamaret.

## Louis Bovard

A Lavaux estate (the steeply terraced UNESCO-listed shore of Lake
Geneva). The Dézaley Chasselas bottlings — particularly La
Médinette — are among the most precise expressions of the
appellation, with serious ageing potential.

## Provins

The largest cooperative-style producer in Valais, with 3,000
member growers. The single-vineyard Maître de Chais bottlings
demonstrate that the cooperative model can produce wines at the
top of the appellation.

## Domaine de la Capitaine

A Vaud estate near Begnins, biodynamic, family-run. The Chasselas
and Pinot Noir bottlings have built a strong following in the
domestic market and are among the most respected biodynamic
Swiss wines.

## Domaine Cornulus

A Valais estate near Savièse. The Heida (the local Savagnin) and
the Petite Arvine are reference bottlings of two of Valais's most
distinctive native varieties.

## Christian Zündel

A Ticino estate, primarily Merlot. Zündel's Orizzonte Merlot is
one of the most respected Ticino reds and helped establish Ticino
Merlot as a serious appellation alongside its older Italian and
French neighbours.

## Tamborini

A larger Ticino estate, owned by the Tamborini family for several
generations. The Comano range and the limited Vinattieri reds are
the standard-bearers; the estate also makes a successful
traditional-method sparkling wine.

## Visiting Switzerland

Sion (Valais), Lausanne (Vaud), and Lugano (Ticino) are the
natural bases. Many estates open small Vinotheks for visitors and
the train network reaches every wine region directly. Find them
