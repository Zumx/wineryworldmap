Chardonnay is the most planted white grape on Earth and the most
malleable — capable of reductive flint in Chablis, oxidative honey in
the Jura, taut citrus in Tasmania, and creamy oak in Sonoma. The
estates below have built their reputations almost entirely on this
one variety.

## Burgundy: Côte de Beaune

The benchmark. Domaine Leflaive (Puligny-Montrachet) and Domaine
Coche-Dury (Meursault) are the two most uncompromising names. Domaine
des Comtes Lafon, Jean-François Coche-Dury's neighbours, and Pierre-
Yves Colin-Morey set the modern standard. Maison Louis Jadot's white
Burgundy book is the most accessible window into the region.

## Chablis

A different Chardonnay altogether — minerally, lean, oysters-and-
Sancerre cool. Raveneau and Dauvissat are the cult names; François
Pinon's neighbour Vincent Dauvissat in particular. William Fèvre,
Domaine Christian Moreau, and Domaine Louis Michel are the
accessible references for Chablis grand cru sites — Les Clos,
Vaudésir, Valmur.

## Champagne (Blanc de Blancs)

The chalkiest expression of Chardonnay. Salon (vintage-only Le Mesnil
Blanc de Blancs) is the apex; Krug Clos du Mesnil and Taittinger
Comtes de Champagne are the prestige expressions. Pierre Péters and
Agrapart are the grower references on the Côte des Blancs.

## California

Kistler Vineyards (Sonoma) is the reference for site-driven
California Chardonnay; Marcassin (Sonoma Coast) for cult mailing-
list bottlings; Far Niente, Stony Hill, and Mayacamas for long-aged
Napa expressions. Hudson, Hyde, and Sangiacomo are key vineyard
sources.

## Australia and Tasmania

Australian Chardonnay reinvented itself in the 2000s with a leaner,
flinty Margaret River and Tasmania style. Leeuwin Estate (Art
Series), Cullen (Kevin John), Vasse Felix Heytesbury, and Tolpuddle
(Tasmania) are reference wines. Mac Forbes and Giant Steps in the
Yarra Valley lead the cool-climate Victorian wave.

## Tasmania

A region built largely around Chardonnay and Pinot Noir. Tolpuddle
and Stoney Rise are reference Chardonnays; Bay of Fires and Pirie
sparkling extend the variety into traditional-method.

## New Zealand

Kumeu River (West Auckland) is one of the world's most highly
regarded Chardonnay specialists; Felton Road in Central Otago is
better known for Pinot but produces excellent Chardonnay too.
