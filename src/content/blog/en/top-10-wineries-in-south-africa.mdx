South African wine has one of the most tumultuous histories in the world. Three centuries of production under the Cape Dutch colonial economy were followed by decades of international isolation during the apartheid era, then a rapid quality revolution after 1994 that has produced, in thirty years, wines competing at the highest international levels. The Cape Winelands — Stellenbosch, Paarl, Franschhoek, and the Swartland — offer a diversity of grape varieties, soil types, and climatic influences that makes South Africa one of the most interesting wine countries to explore. At the centre of it all is Pinotage: a crossing of Cinsault and Pinot Noir created at Stellenbosch University in 1925, adopted as South Africa's national grape, and capable, in the hands of the best producers, of wines of genuine world-class distinction.

## 1. Vergelegen, Stellenbosch

Vergelegen's story begins in 1700 when Willem Adriaan van der Stel, Governor of the Cape Colony, established the estate at Somerset West on the Hottentots Holland mountains. The Cape Dutch manor house, historic camphor trees (designated national monuments), and formal gardens are among the finest heritage attractions in the Cape Winelands, and the wines match the historical significance. The Vergelegen V Red (Cabernet Sauvignon-Merlot-Cabernet Franc-Petit Verdot-Malbec Bordeaux blend) is the prestige flagship, consistently among South Africa's finest reds. The Vergelegen White (Sauvignon Blanc-Semillon) and Vergelegen Chardonnay are equally serious. The biodynamically farmed estate vineyard produces fruit of considerable concentration, and the 2009, 2012, and 2015 V Red are the modern benchmarks.

## 2. Klein Constantia, Constantia

Klein Constantia occupies a portion of the historical Groot Constantia estate — the oldest wine estate in the Cape, established by Simon van der Stel in 1685. The Vin de Constance, a sweet Muscat de Frontignan wine reviving the 18th-century Constantia wine praised by Napoleon, Jane Austen, and Frederick the Great, is one of South Africa's most internationally recognised wines and one of the world's great dessert wines. The 500ml half-bottle expression and the half-litre bottle have become design icons. Klein Constantia also produces dry wines — the KC Sauvignon Blanc, Perdeblokke Sauvignon Blanc, and the Metis range (in collaboration with Dourthe of Bordeaux) — from the cool, maritime-influenced Constantia Valley.

## 3. Kanonkop, Stellenbosch

Kanonkop is the most important estate in the history of Pinotage. The Black Label Pinotage — produced from 40-year-old bush-vine Pinotage on iron-rich granite soils in the Simonsberg-Stellenbosch appellation — is consistently voted among South Africa's best wines and has been Kanonkop's prestige expression since the 1970s under legendary winemaker Jan "Boland" Coetzee and subsequently under Abrie Beeslaar. The Paul Sauer (Cabernet Sauvignon-Cabernet Franc-Merlot, inspired by Bordeaux) is the flagship Bordeaux blend and one of the Cape's most age-worthy reds. The Kadette range (Pinotage and Cape Blend blends) offers accessible Kanonkop quality. The estate's commitment to old-vine Pinotage makes it the reference producer for anyone seeking to understand the variety at its most serious.

## 4. Boekenhoutskloof, Franschhoek

Marc Kent's Boekenhoutskloof, established in 1776 as a farm and modern as a quality wine estate since 1994, produces some of the Cape's most critically discussed wines. The Boekenhoutskloof Syrah (from Franschhoek) is the prestige expression — one of South Africa's great Syrahahs, powerful, gamey, and structured. The Boekenhoutskloof Cabernet Sauvignon and the Chocolate Block (a Syrah-dominant blend, named for the chocolate-rich soil of the Franschhoek vineyards) are the widely distributed flagships. The Wolftrap range provides excellent value, and the Porcupine Ridge label extends the estate's quality at the accessible end. The Franschhoek estate, surrounded by mountains, is one of the Cape's most spectacular settings.

## 5. Mullineux, Swartland

Chris and Andrea Mullineux established their estate in Riebeek-Kasteel in the Swartland in 2007, and within a decade had produced a run of wines — particularly old-vine Syrah and Chenin Blanc from schist, granite, and iron-rich soils — that placed the Swartland on the international fine wine map. The Mullineux Schist Syrah, Granite Syrah, and Iron Syrah are single-soil-type expressions of a single variety that demonstrate how profoundly terroir affects expression. The Straw Wine (Chenin Blanc, made from sun-dried grapes) and the White (Chenin Blanc) are important expressions of the variety the Swartland has adopted as its own. Andrea Mullineux was named Winemaker of the Year at Platter's South African Wine Guide twice in succession — a recognition of the estate's significance to South African wine's continued evolution.

## 6. Beyerskloof, Stellenbosch

Beyers Truter, who produced the historic Kanonkop Pinotage vintages of the 1980s and 1990s as winemaker before establishing his own estate, is the most important individual figure in the history of South African Pinotage. Beyerskloof, established in 1988 in the Simonsberg valley, produces the Cape's most important range of single-variety Pinotage: the Beyerskloof Field Blend (the prestige expression), the Reserve Pinotage, and the Synergy Cape Blend (Pinotage-Cabernet Sauvignon-Merlot). The estate's work in demonstrating the variety's quality potential — and in organising the Cape Pinotage Association that codified farming and winemaking standards — has been essential to Pinotage's rehabilitation as a serious wine variety internationally.

## 7. Hamilton Russell Vineyards, Hemel-en-Aarde

Anthony Hamilton Russell established his estate in the Hemel-en-Aarde Valley near Hermanus in 1976, correctly identifying that the cool, maritime valley closest to the Southern Ocean would allow Pinot Noir and Chardonnay to ripen at Burgundy-level temperatures. The Hamilton Russell Pinot Noir and Chardonnay are consistently ranked among the finest produced in the southern hemisphere: structured, fine-grained, mineral, and genuine in their Burgundian ambition without being mere imitations. The 2012, 2015, and 2019 Pinot Noirs are the modern reference vintages. The Hemel-en-Aarde sub-appellation has since attracted Storm, Creation, and dozens of other producers drawn by the Burgundian template that Hamilton Russell established.

## 8. Meerlust, Stellenbosch

Meerlust, one of the oldest continuously family-owned estates in the Cape (the Myburgh family has owned it since 1757), produces Rubicon — the Cape's original Bordeaux blend — from vines on the Faure flats between Stellenbosch and False Bay. First released in 1980, Rubicon was among the first Cape wines to demonstrate that South African reds could age and develop with Bordeaux-inspired complexity over two decades. The Meerlust Chardonnay and the Pinot Noir are equally serious, the latter demonstrating that coastal Stellenbosch can produce elegant rather than heavy Pinot. The 2004 and 2009 Rubicon vintages are widely regarded as the finest in the modern era.

## 9. Rust en Vrede, Stellenbosch

Rust en Vrede (Peace and Rest) in the Stellenbosch Appellation is a specialist red wine estate producing nothing but red wine — an unusual commitment in the Cape. The Estate Wine (Cabernet Sauvignon-Shiraz-Merlot blend), produced since 1979, is a Cape classic of consistent quality. The 1694 Classification (a single wine, the estate's ultimate expression, blended from the best barrels) and the single-variety Cabernet Sauvignon and Shiraz represent the prestige tier. Winemaker Coenie Snyman has maintained the estate's commitment to Syrah as a serious variety in the Stellenbosch warmth, and the Rust en Vrede Syrah is consistently one of the Cape's finest expressions.

## 10. Tokara, Stellenbosch and Elgin

Tokara (named for a combination of the owners' children's names) sits high on the Helshoogte Pass between Stellenbosch and Franschhoek, with views over both valleys and a spectacular elevation that cools the vineyards significantly. The Director's Reserve Red (Cabernet Sauvignon-Shiraz blend) and Director's Reserve White (Sauvignon Blanc-Semillon) are the prestige expressions; the Tokara Zondernaam range offers excellent value from the same estate. The Tokara Walker Bay Elgin Chardonnay and Elgin Sauvignon Blanc, from fruit sourced in the cool Elgin appellation, demonstrate the estate's ambition across South Africa's diverse appellations. The Tokara restaurant and deli on the Helshoogte Pass is one of the Cape's finest winery dining experiences.

## Pinotage: South Africa's National Grape

Pinotage was created in 1925 by Professor Abraham Perold at Stellenbosch University through a crossing of Cinsault (then called Hermitage in South Africa, hence the name) and Pinot Noir. The first commercial wine was produced in 1941 and the variety achieved widespread planting through the 1950s and 1960s. Its reputation suffered in the 1970s and 1980s from poor winemaking (excessive heat fermentation producing an acetone-and-rust-nail character) before a quality revolution led by Beyers Truter and Kanonkop demonstrated what old-vine, low-yield Pinotage from good soils could achieve. At its best — Kanonkop Black Label, Beyerskloof Field Blend — Pinotage produces wines of plum fruit, dark chocolate, coffee, and earthy complexity unlike anything else on Earth.
