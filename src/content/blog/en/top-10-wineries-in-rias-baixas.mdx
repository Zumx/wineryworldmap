Rías Baixas is the most distinctive Atlantic appellation in Spain.
The region sits on the Galician coast, where deep, drowned river
valleys (the rías) carve into granite, the climate is mild and very
wet by Spanish standards, and the local grape — Albariño — produces
a saline, citrus-driven white wine that bears almost no resemblance
to the broader Spanish wine style. The vines are traditionally
trained on pergolas (parras) to lift the fruit off the damp ground,
giving the vineyards a distinctive look.

## Pazo de Señoráns

Founded by Marisol Bueno (a former president of the appellation) in
1989. The estate Albariño is among the most precise in the region;
the Selección de Añada, a long-aged Albariño released after several
years, demonstrated that Albariño can age beautifully — a
revelation in a region long sold only as a young wine.

## Bodegas Zárate

The Zárate family has farmed the Salnés Valley vineyards for
several generations. Their entry Albariño and the single-vineyard
El Palomar are benchmarks of the unoaked, mineral, salt-driven
style. They also produce reds from the obscure local Caíño Tinto.

## Pazo de Barrantes

Owned by the Marqués de Murrieta (one of the great Rioja houses),
the Pazo de Barrantes estate produces a fuller-bodied, oak-influenced
Albariño that stands apart from the unoaked majority. La Comtesse,
the prestige cuvée, has serious ageing potential.

## Forjas del Salnés

Rodrigo Méndez's small estate. The Leirana Albariño is one of the
most highly regarded in the appellation; the Goliardo single-vineyard
wines (both white and red) show how distinctive the Salnés terroirs
can be when worked precisely.

## Bodegas Valdamor

A reliable mid-sized producer in the Salnés Valley. The estate
Albariño is a standard reference for the region's friendly,
fruit-forward style, and a useful first introduction for anyone
new to Albariño.

## Adegas Galegas

Larger commercial winery producing well-regarded everyday Albariño
under several labels (Don Pedro de Soutomaior, Veigadares). The
Selección Especial bottlings show what the operation can do at the
top of its range.

## Granbazán

The estate dates to the 19th century but the modern wines have been
shaped by extensive vineyard work in the last twenty years. The
Etiqueta Verde and Etiqueta Ámbar bottlings are well-respected
entry-level Albariños; the Limousin is a barrel-aged expression.

## Lagar de Cervera

Owned by La Rioja Alta (another major Rioja house), Lagar de Cervera
is a long-established producer of clean, fruit-driven Albariño from
the O Rosal sub-zone, which sits along the Miño river on the
Portuguese border.

## Terras Gauda

A major estate in the O Rosal sub-zone, producing a multi-variety
blend (Albariño with Loureiro and Caíño Blanco) that is one of the
most successful field blends in Spanish white wine. The Black Label
Etiqueta Negra is the prestige cuvée.

## Quinta de Couselo

Owner-grower estate in the O Rosal sub-zone. The Selección
Especial bottling is barrel-aged Albariño with the region's
characteristic salinity but additional complexity from oak.

## Visiting Rías Baixas

The appellation spreads across five sub-zones; the most visited are
the Salnés Valley (around Cambados) and O Rosal (along the Miño).
