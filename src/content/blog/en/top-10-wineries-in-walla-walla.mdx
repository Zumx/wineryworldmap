Walla Walla sits in the dry south-east corner of Washington State,
where the Blue Mountains shelter a former wheat valley that has
quietly become the most ambitious wine region in the Pacific
Northwest. The first modern vines went in during the late 1970s;
today the AVA spans the Oregon-Washington border, and the cobblestone
Rocks District below Milton-Freewater has emerged as one of the most
distinctive Syrah terroirs in the New World.

## Leonetti Cellar

The estate that started it all. Gary Figgins planted in 1977 and
released the first commercial Walla Walla Cabernet a few vintages
later — wines that, by the mid-1990s, were trading on auction at
prices once reserved for Napa cult bottlings. Still family-run, still
the regional benchmark.

## Cayuse Vineyards

Christophe Baron, a transplanted Champenois, planted Syrah into the
Rocks District basalt cobbles in 1997 when locals thought he was
mad. The wines — En Cerise, Bionic Frog, Armada — defined the
savoury, meat-and-stone Walla Walla Syrah style and now sell only
through a multi-year mailing list.

## Reynvaan Family Vineyards

The Reynvaan family farms two estate vineyards: the cobblestone In
the Rocks site for Syrah and the higher-elevation Foothills Reserve
for Cabernet and Bordeaux blends. Mike and Gary Reynvaan are
considered Cayuse's stylistic heirs in the Rocks.

## No Girls / K Vintners

Charles Smith's K Vintners and the smaller No Girls label deliver
some of the most expressive Syrahs in the AVA, including the
single-vineyard Royal City. Smith's punkish marketing belies
serious wines from old-vine sites.

## Woodward Canyon

Founded 1981 by Rick Small, Woodward Canyon's Old Vines Cabernet
and Estate Reserve are among the longest-running benchmark
Bordeaux-style wines in the region. The Lowden tasting room is one
of the older addresses in Walla Walla.

## L'Ecole Nº 41

Housed in a 1915 schoolhouse outside the town of Lowden — hence the
name. Three generations of the Clubb family have built a broad,
consistently excellent portfolio, with the Ferguson Vineyard
Cabernet/Merlot blend as flagship.

## Pepper Bridge

The estate's Seven Hills Vineyard, jointly developed with Leonetti
and Figgins, is the most famous single vineyard in Walla Walla.
Pepper Bridge's own Cabernet and Trine (a Bordeaux blend) showcase
the Seven Hills terroir at the upper end of the AVA.

## Figgins Family Wine Estates

A separate label from Chris Figgins (Gary's son), focused on
single-vineyard Estate Red blends from high-elevation Estate
Vineyard. Lyle Cabernet and Toil Pinot Noir round out the range.

## Doubleback

Drew Bledsoe's wine project. McQueen Cabernet and Bledsoe Family
Estate wines come from McQueen and Bob Healy vineyards. Bledsoe
hired Chris Figgins as consulting winemaker — the seriousness shows
in the bottle.

## Gramercy Cellars

Master Sommelier Greg Harrington's project. Old World-leaning
Syrahs (Lagniappe, Forgotten Hills) and Bordeaux blends with a
restrained, savoury house style. Among the most respected newer
addresses in the AVA.

## Visiting Walla Walla

Walla Walla town has a small regional airport with direct flights
from Seattle and Portland. Tasting rooms cluster downtown and
along the Old Highway 12 corridor. The Rocks District is a short
drive south across the Oregon line. Find every estate on the
