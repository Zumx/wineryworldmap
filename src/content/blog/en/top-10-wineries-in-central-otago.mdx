Central Otago sits on the inland side of New Zealand's South Island,
in the lee of the Southern Alps. It is one of the world's southernmost
significant wine regions, at the 45th parallel south. The climate is
continental — hot dry summers, cold winters with frost — which is
unusual for a region surrounded by ocean and which gives Central
Otago's wines a structure and depth that the more maritime Marlborough,
to the north, cannot reach. The signature grape is Pinot Noir, planted
on schist-derived soils across six sub-regions: Gibbston, Bannockburn,
Cromwell-Lowburn-Pisa, Bendigo, Wanaka, and Alexandra.

## Felton Road

Founded by Stewart Elms in 1991, now run by Nigel Greening. The
Bannockburn estate is biodynamic and has developed a reputation for
some of the most precise New Zealand Pinot Noir. Block 3 and Block 5
single-vineyard wines are reference points.

## Rippon

A pioneering estate on the shore of Lake Wanaka, biodynamic since
its founding. Nick Mills produces some of the most distinctive
Central Otago Pinot Noir, including a single-vineyard Mature Vine
Pinot from vines planted in the 1980s.

## Mount Difficulty

A larger producer in Bannockburn with a strong reputation for
single-vineyard wines (Long Gully, Pipeclay Terrace, Target Gully).
The cellar door has a commanding view over the Cromwell Basin and
draws many regional visitors.

## Burn Cottage

A biodynamic estate in Cromwell co-founded by US wine importer Marquis
Sauvage and consulting winemaker Ted Lemon (of California's Littorai).
The wines are restrained, precise, and high in tension.

## Mount Edward

Founded in 1997 by Alan Brady (one of the Central Otago founders) and
now run by Duncan Forsyth. The estate focuses on Pinot Noir from
distinct Gibbston, Pisa, and Bannockburn vineyards under separate
labels.

## Two Paddocks

The estate of actor Sam Neill (of Jurassic Park fame). Two Paddocks
sits across four small Central Otago vineyards; the wines have
gained serious critical respect well beyond the celebrity angle.

## Quartz Reef

A Bendigo estate founded by Rudi Bauer in 1996. Quartz Reef makes
both still Pinot Noir and a traditional-method sparkling — one of
New Zealand's most respected méthode traditionnelle producers.

## Akarua

A Bannockburn estate with significant Pinot Noir and sparkling-wine
production. The Akarua Brut and Rua Pinot are reliable mid-range
expressions of the region; the prestige Bannockburn Pinot Noir is
the top wine.

## Carrick

A Bannockburn estate certified organic, known for nervy, energetic
Pinot Noir and a small Pinot Noir-based rosé. The Carrick Bannockburn
Pinot Noir is the standard release; Excelsior is the prestige
cuvée.

## Pyramid Valley

Technically based in North Canterbury, but with significant Central
Otago vineyards and frequent tasting room appearance in the region.
Pyramid Valley is biodynamic and produces some of the most
intellectually engaged Pinot Noir in New Zealand.

## Visiting Central Otago

Queenstown is the natural base — most estates are within an hour's
drive. Bannockburn and Gibbston are the most compact tasting routes;
Wanaka and Alexandra reward longer trips. Find them all on the
