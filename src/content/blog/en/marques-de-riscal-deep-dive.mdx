Marqués de Riscal is the oldest classified estate of the Rioja
Alavesa and one of the most historically important in Spain. Its
founder, Don Camilo Hurtado de Amézaga, returned from France in
the 1850s convinced that Rioja could produce wines on a par with
Bordeaux — and built the estate accordingly.

## The founding

Founded in 1858 in Elciego on the Alavesa bank of the Ebro, Riscal
was the first Spanish estate to systematically import French
techniques. Cabernet Sauvignon was planted alongside Tempranillo;
French and American oak barrels replaced traditional clay; the
wine was bottled and labelled in the modern sense.

## The wines

- **Marqués de Riscal Reserva** — the flagship of the modern
range; Tempranillo with Graciano and Mazuelo. American oak. The
benchmark of the house.
- **Marqués de Riscal Gran Reserva** — released only in great
years, with longer oak and bottle ageing.
- **Barón de Chirel Reserva** — the prestige bottling. Old-vine
Tempranillo with a small percentage of Cabernet Sauvignon —
historically Spain's first commercial wine to feature Cabernet on
the label.
- **Marqués de Riscal Rueda Verdejo** — from the estate's separate
Rueda operation, a reference Spanish Verdejo.

## The Frank Gehry hotel

In 2006, Marqués de Riscal opened the Hotel Marqués de Riscal, a
luxury hotel designed by Frank Gehry — titanium-clad in pink,
gold, and silver, a deliberate reference to the foil of a Riscal
wine bottle. The hotel and restaurant, the Vinothèque, and the
historic cellars together form the City of Wine — one of the
most polished wine-tourism destinations in Spain.

## The 1928 vintage

A particular Riscal vintage — the 1928 — is celebrated for the
estate's defining moment. It was widely served at the wedding of
the Spanish king and helped establish Riscal as the country's
luxury wine.

## The historic library

Riscal's underground cellar holds bottles from every vintage back
to the founding — a *cathedral* (their term) of more than 130,000
bottles. The collection is one of the most valuable in Europe.

## Visiting

Marqués de Riscal welcomes visitors. The estate runs daily tours of
the cellar, the historic library, and the Frank Gehry hotel
building; the on-site Michelin-starred restaurant draws diners
from across Spain. Book ahead, especially in the summer months.
