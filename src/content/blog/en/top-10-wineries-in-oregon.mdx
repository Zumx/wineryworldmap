Oregon makes some of the finest Pinot Noir outside Burgundy, and it
does so in a cool, marginal climate that the state's pioneers chose
precisely because it resembled the Côte d'Or. The Willamette Valley,
south of Portland, is the heart — a patchwork of volcanic Jory soils
and marine sedimentary slopes where Pinot Noir and Chardonnay express
nuance over power. From the founding generation of the 1960s and 70s
to the Burgundians who have arrived since, these ten estates lead the
state.

## 1. Domaine Drouhin Oregon, Dundee Hills

The Burgundian house Joseph Drouhin planted in the Dundee Hills in
1987 — a defining vote of confidence in Oregon Pinot Noir. Veronique
Drouhin makes elegant, ageworthy Pinot (Laurène, Louise) and
Chardonnay (Arthur, Édition Limitée) in a precise Burgundian style.

## 2. The Eyrie Vineyards, Dundee

David Lett — "Papa Pinot" — planted Oregon's first Pinot Noir and
Pinot Gris here in 1965, founding the state's fine-wine history. The
1975 South Block Pinot Noir famously ranked among top Burgundies in a
1979 Paris tasting. Now run by his son Jason.

## 3. Beaux Frères, Ribbon Ridge

Founded in 1986 with the involvement of critic Robert Parker, Beaux
Frères built a reputation for rich, concentrated Pinot Noir from its
biodynamic Ribbon Ridge estate. A benchmark of the fuller Oregon
style.

## 4. Bergström Wines, Dundee Hills

A biodynamic family estate making refined, mineral Pinot Noir and
some of Oregon's most admired Chardonnay (Sigrid). The single-vineyard
Pinots — Bergström, Shea, Le Pré du Col — show the range of valley
terroirs.

## 5. Cristom Vineyards, Eola-Amity Hills

A specialist in whole-cluster Pinot Noir from its windswept
Eola-Amity hillsides, with named vineyards honouring family women —
Marjorie, Jessie, Eileen. Among the most consistent and distinctive
estates in the state.

## 6. Domaine Serene, Dundee Hills

A high-profile producer of polished, oak-influenced Pinot Noir
(Evenstad Reserve) and rich Chardonnay (Recolte). Its hilltop winery
is among Oregon's most ambitious visitor destinations.

## 7. Ponzi Vineyards, Beaverton/Sherwood

Founded in 1970 by Dick and Nancy Ponzi, one of the pioneer
families, with a long record of refined Pinot Noir and pioneering
work with Arneis and other Italian varieties. A cornerstone of the
valley.

## 8. Adelsheim Vineyard, Chehalem Mountains

Founded in 1971 by David Adelsheim, a tireless ambassador who helped
write Oregon's labelling laws. The estate makes graceful Pinot Noir
and was an early champion of the Chehalem Mountains as a fine-wine
sub-region.

## 9. Evening Land Vineyards, Eola-Amity Hills

Centred on the historic Seven Springs Vineyard, now made with
Burgundian winemaker Sashi Moorman and sommelier Rajat Parr. Its
Summum and Seven Springs Pinots and Chardonnays are among Oregon's
most sought-after.

## 10. Bethel Heights Vineyard, Eola-Amity Hills

A pioneering Eola-Amity estate planted in 1977, family-run and
celebrated for site-expressive Pinot Noir from its estate blocks.
A quietly excellent producer central to the sub-region's reputation.

## Oregon's appellations

The Willamette Valley AVA is divided into a growing set of nested
sub-AVAs, each with a distinct soil signature. The Dundee Hills, on
red volcanic Jory soil, give perfumed, red-fruited Pinot. The
Eola-Amity Hills, cooled by winds through the Van Duzer Corridor,
give structured, savoury wines. Ribbon Ridge, the Chehalem
Mountains, Yamhill-Carlton and McMinnville complete the picture.
Beyond the Willamette, the Rogue and Umpqua valleys to the south grow
warmer-climate varieties.

## Visiting Oregon wine country

The Willamette Valley is an easy hour's drive southwest of Portland,
and the towns of Dundee, Newberg and McMinnville make ideal bases.
Tasting rooms are welcoming and far less formal than Napa, often with
the winemaker pouring. The International Pinot Noir Celebration in
McMinnville each summer is the state's great wine gathering. Autumn
harvest is the most atmospheric time to visit.
