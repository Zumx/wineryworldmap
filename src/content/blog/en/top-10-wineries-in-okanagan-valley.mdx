The Okanagan Valley stretches roughly 250 km south from Kelowna,
British Columbia, to the U.S. border at Osoyoos. The valley floor is
narrow, the lake-lined slopes are steep, and the southern third is
genuinely desert — Canada's only true pocket-desert ecosystem. Within
this strip the climate moves from cool-climate Riesling and Pinot
Noir in the north to ripe Syrah and Bordeaux blends in the south,
and a few of Canada's most ambitious wineries have set the tone for
the broader Pacific Northwest.

## Mission Hill Family Estate

The most architecturally imposing winery in Canada — Anthony von
Mandl's hilltop estate above Westbank, with its bell tower and
underground barrel cellar. The Oculus Bordeaux blend has been a
Canadian benchmark since the late 1990s; the Quatrain and Compendium
single-vineyard wines deserve more international attention than they
get.

## CedarCreek Estate Winery

Also part of the von Mandl group, on the east shore of Okanagan Lake.
Certified organic, biodynamic-adjacent farming, and a sophisticated
Pinot Noir programme led by winemaker Taylor Whelan that competes
with anything west of the Rockies.

## Burrowing Owl Estate Winery

Hilltop in the desert south, overlooking Osoyoos Lake. Burrowing
Owl pioneered seriously ripe Cabernet, Merlot, and Syrah in Canada
in the late 1990s. The on-site Sonora Room restaurant and
guesthouse remain the most polished wine-tourism address in the
south Okanagan.

## Osoyoos Larose

A joint venture between Vincor and Groupe Taillan (Château
Gruaud-Larose). The single-wine estate produces only one bottling
— Le Grand Vin, a Bordeaux blend grown on the bench above
Osoyoos — and was for years the South Okanagan's most credible
international ambassador.

## Tantalus Vineyards

Old-vine Riesling specialist on Kelowna's Pioneer Block, one of
the oldest continuously planted Riesling sites in the Okanagan.
Owner Eric Savics and winemaker David Paterson make some of the
most ageworthy Rieslings outside the Mosel.

## Quails' Gate Estate Winery

Family-run in West Kelowna by the Stewart family. The Stewart
Family Reserve Pinot Noir and the Connemara Estate range are
benchmarks for the north Okanagan; the lake-side restaurant is a
fixture of Kelowna wine tourism.

## Le Vieux Pin

In Oliver, in the south. Severine Pinte makes Rhône-leaning
Syrah and Roussanne/Marsanne that compete on quality with Walla
Walla and the northern Rhône itself. LVP Equinoxe Syrah is
intermittently brilliant.

## Black Hills Estate Winery

On the Black Sage Bench, near Oliver. Nota Bene, a Bordeaux blend,
was one of Canada's first cult wines on release — for a decade
the estate sold its entire production on a single Saturday allocation
day.

## Painted Rock Estate Winery

A more recent (2007) estate on the Skaha Bluffs above Penticton.
Bordeaux-blend reds (Red Icon) and Chardonnay from a single
hillside vineyard. Considered one of the South Okanagan's most
quality-driven newer projects.

## Inniskillin Okanagan

The Okanagan satellite of Niagara's icewine pioneer. Inniskillin
Okanagan's Riesling and Vidal icewines compete with Niagara's at
the top of the Canadian icewine category — a winter-vintage
specialty that few other countries can produce reliably.

## Visiting the Okanagan

Kelowna has the regional airport and most of the north Okanagan
wineries; Penticton, Naramata, Oliver, and Osoyoos string out
southward by car (about two hours end to end). The Naramata Bench
above Penticton concentrates small-producer wineries along a single
walkable strip. Find every estate on the [interactive map](/map) —
filter to Okanagan.
