Stellenbosch is the historic heart of South African wine — a town and a
wine district 50 km east of Cape Town, set against the Helderberg,
Simonsberg, and Stellenbosch mountains. Cabernet Sauvignon-led
Bordeaux blends are the regional flagship; Chenin Blanc, Pinotage, and
Cape White blends round out the picture. These ten estates lead.

## 1. Kanonkop, Stellenbosch (Simonsberg)

The reference for Pinotage and an exemplary Bordeaux blend with Paul
Sauer. One of South Africa's most consistently respected estates,
with deep cellar tradition.

## 2. Vergelegen, Somerset West (Helderberg)

A historic 17th-century estate restored by Anglo American. Vergelegen
Estate (the Bordeaux red blend) and the V single-vineyard wines are
South African references; the gardens and visitor experience are
exceptional.

## 3. Meerlust, Stellenbosch

The other historic Bordeaux-blend benchmark. Rubicon — a Cabernet,
Merlot, Cabernet Franc, Petit Verdot blend — has been a flagship of
Cape wine since the 1980s.

## 4. Rust en Vrede, Stellenbosch (Helderberg)

A red-only estate making Cabernet, Syrah, and the Estate Bordeaux
blend; Single Vineyard Cabernet Sauvignon is the prestige bottle.

## 5. Tokara, Stellenbosch

A modern estate at the top of the Helshoogte Pass with both wine and
olive programmes; Reserve Collection Director's Reserve White and Red
are reference Cape wines.

## 6. Boekenhoutskloof, Franschhoek (technically just outside Stellenbosch)

Marc Kent's estate making Syrah, Cabernet, and Semillon; the
Boekenhoutskloof Syrah and the Cabernet are among South Africa's most
collectible wines.

## 7. Mulderbosch, Stellenbosch

The reference for Cape Chenin Blanc and Sauvignon Blanc, with strong
Cabernet bottlings from Faithful Hound; pioneer of single-vineyard
Chenin in South Africa.

## 8. Klein Constantia (technically Constantia, not Stellenbosch — but close)

The historic estate that produced Vin de Constance, the world-famous
sweet wine of the 18th century, revived in 1986. Among the great
sweet wines of the world.

## 9. De Toren, Stellenbosch

A boutique estate producing Fusion V (a five-Bordeaux-variety blend)
and Z (single-vineyard); among the most modern, polished red wine
programmes in the Cape.

## 10. Warwick Estate, Stellenbosch

A family estate famous for Trilogy (Bordeaux blend) and Three Cape
Ladies (Bordeaux-Pinotage blend); welcoming visitor programme and a
strong restaurant.

## Visiting Stellenbosch

Stellenbosch town is a perfect base — handsome Cape Dutch
architecture, dozens of estates within fifteen minutes' drive. Most
have tasting rooms open daily; many include restaurants, art
collections, and gardens. February-March is harvest; May-August is
quiet and beautiful.
