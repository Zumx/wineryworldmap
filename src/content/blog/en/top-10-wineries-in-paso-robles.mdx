Paso Robles is the most diverse wine region in California's Central
Coast. The appellation, split by the US 101 between cool, foggy
limestone hills to the west and warmer alluvial valleys to the east,
has 11 sub-AVAs and grows everything from Cabernet to Rhône
varietals to Italian-influenced field blends. The region's modern
profile was shaped by a handful of pioneers in the late 20th century;
the boom of small estates since 2010 has lifted Paso to the front
rank of American wine regions.

## Tablas Creek

Founded as a partnership between the Perrin family (of Château de
Beaucastel in Châteauneuf-du-Pape) and importer Robert Haas. Tablas
Creek pioneered the use of authentic Rhône grape clones in
California and became the source of much of the Rhône-variety
material now planted across Paso. The Esprit de Tablas red and
white are reference points.

## Saxum

Justin Smith's tiny estate on the west side. The James Berry
Vineyard bottlings — particularly the Heart Stone Mourvèdre — are
among the most sought-after California wines, and Saxum is a
former Wine Spectator Wine of the Year producer.

## Linne Calodo

Matt Trevisan's estate, founded in 1998. The names of the wines —
"Cherry Red", "Outsider", "Sticks and Stones" — reference the
limestone "calodo" soils. The wines are blends of Rhône varieties
with Zinfandel; the style is rich and powerful.

## Adelaida

A historic west-side estate dating to the 1960s, now run by the
Van Steenwyk family. Adelaida grows everything from Pinot Noir
(unusual for Paso) to Mourvèdre. The Reserve Pinot Noir from the
Anna's Vineyard is a benchmark.

## Justin Vineyards

The largest estate on the west side, founded by Justin Baldwin in
1981 and now owned by the Resnick family (FIJI Water, POM). The
Isosceles Bordeaux blend put Paso Robles Cabernet on the global map
in the 1990s.

## L'Aventure

Founded by Frenchman Stéphan Asseo in 1998, who moved from Bordeaux
to make wines that combine Rhône and Bordeaux varieties in ways
not permitted under French appellation law. The Estate Cuvée and
Côte à Côte are flagship blends; the Optimus is a cellar-door
favourite.

## Booker Vineyard

Eric Jensen's small estate. The Vertigo, Fracture (Mourvèdre), and
White (Roussanne) are limited-production bottlings; the style is
intense and well-extracted. Booker's mailing list is essentially
the only retail route.

## Daou Vineyards

A larger, more international-style estate founded in 2007 by the
brothers Daniel and Georges Daou. The Soul of a Lion Bordeaux blend
is the prestige cuvée; the cellar door is one of the most polished
visitor experiences in the appellation.

## Halter Ranch

A west-side estate with both estate wine production and a 280-acre
vineyard. The Ancestor red blend has built a strong reputation; the
visitor centre is one of the more architecturally significant
recent additions to Paso.

## Jada Vineyard

Founded by Jack Messina in 2003. The Hell's Kitchen (Cabernet
Sauvignon), Passing By (white blend), and XCV (red blend) are the
range; the Passing By Roussanne-Viognier set the modern Paso
white-blend template.

## Visiting Paso Robles

The appellation is large (over 600,000 acres); plan to focus on
either the west side (limestone, cool, Rhône-leaning) or the east
side (alluvial, warmer, Cabernet-and-Zinfandel-leaning) per
visit. Find every estate on the [interactive map](/map) — filter
