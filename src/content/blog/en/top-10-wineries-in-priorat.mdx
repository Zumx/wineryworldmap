Priorat is the most dramatic landscape in Spanish wine. Steep,
terraced slate slopes (the local llicorella) rise behind the village
of Gratallops in Catalonia, with vineyards that were nearly abandoned
in the 1980s after a century of decline. Five outsiders — René
Barbier, Álvaro Palacios, Carles Pastrana, José Luis Pérez, and
Daphne Glorian — settled in the village in 1989, replanted the
abandoned bush vines, and within a decade had created some of the
most sought-after red wines in the world.

## Álvaro Palacios

Álvaro Palacios, son of the Rioja family of the same name, was one
of the original five. His L'Ermita, from a tiny parcel of
century-old Garnacha vines, is one of Spain's most expensive wines.
His Finca Dofí and Les Terrasses are more accessible expressions of
the same estate philosophy.

## Clos Mogador

René Barbier's estate; his Clos Mogador is widely considered the
prototypical modern Priorat. The wine is a blend of Garnacha,
Cariñena, Syrah, and Cabernet Sauvignon, fermented in open vats and
aged in mostly French oak. The estate is biodynamic.

## Clos Erasmus

Daphne Glorian's tiny estate. The Clos Erasmus, from a single
hillside vineyard above Gratallops, is made only in vintages of
sufficient quality. The wine has a cult following and is one of the
hardest Priorat wines to find at retail.

## Clos Martinet

Carles Pastrana's estate. The Clos Martinet is a Garnacha-Cariñena
blend with the small bush vines of the original terraces; the second
wine, Menut, offers a more accessible introduction to the style.

## Mas Martinet

José Luis Pérez's estate, now run by his daughter Sara Pérez. The
Mas Martinet Clàssic and Bru bottlings are among the most precise
expressions of the appellation. The estate is biodynamic.

## Vall Llach

Founded by the Catalan singer Lluís Llach in 1999. The Vall Llach
Embruix has become an entry point to the appellation; the top wine,
Idus, has the depth and structure expected of the village.

## Costers del Siurana

Carles Pastrana's parallel estate to Clos Martinet. The Clos de
l'Obac and Miserere bottlings have been the estate's standard-bearers
for over three decades.

## Terroir al Límit

Dominik Huber's small estate, founded with Eben Sadie in 2001
(Sadie is no longer involved). The wines — Les Manyes, Les Tosses
— are made from old-vine Garnacha at very high altitude with
minimal-intervention winemaking and reflect a sea-change in how
Priorat can be expressed.

## Ferrer Bobet

A modern estate founded in 1999 by Sergi Ferrer-Salat and Raül
Bobet. The vineyards are at higher altitude than the historic core
of the appellation, and the wines have a noticeably fresher and
more aromatic profile than the deeper, denser wines of the village
parcels.

## Scala Dei

The oldest active winery in Priorat, on the site of the medieval
Carthusian monastery that gave the appellation its name (Scala
Dei = the Latin name of the monastery, from which the medieval
"priorat" was administered). The wines have been remade in recent
vintages and now rank among the appellation's best.

## Visiting Priorat

Gratallops, Porrera, and Torroja del Priorat are the central
villages; most estates offer tastings by appointment. The drive
from Barcelona is about two hours. Find every Priorat estate on the
