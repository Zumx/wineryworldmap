Alentejo is the great wine landscape of inland Portugal — open
plains, cork-oak savanna (montado), wide skies, and small whitewashed
villages where the talha (the local clay amphora) was used to ferment
wine continuously from Roman times until well into the 20th century.
The modern era has brought stainless steel and oak barrels too, but
several estates still use talha alongside, and Alentejo has become
one of Portugal's most consistent sources of red and white wine in
the mid-to-upper price tier.

## Mouchão

The oldest reference estate of the appellation. Mouchão's tile-roofed
cellar holds large oak vats; the wine, predominantly Alicante
Bouschet with Trincadeira, is famously long-lived. The estate
remained in production through the post-revolution land
reforms — a rare continuity.

## Esporão

Founded in modern form in the 1970s by José Roquette, Esporão is the
largest fine-wine estate in Alentejo. Located near Reguengos de
Monsaraz, the estate produces a huge range — from accessible
"Defesa" wines to the prestige Esporão Private Selection and a
range of vineyard-designated reds.

## Herdade do Mouchão

(Same as Mouchão above; entry consolidated below — see Cortes de
Cima and others for additional historic estates.) The estate
remains the single greatest expression of Alicante Bouschet in
Portugal.

## Cortes de Cima

A modern Alentejo estate founded by Hans Jorgensen, a Dane who
settled in the region in the late 1980s. The Incognito Syrah,
produced from vines that initially had to be sold under a generic
label because Syrah was not yet permitted in DOC Alentejo, became
one of the region's signature wines.

## Herdade do Esporão

Already mentioned, but worth noting separately for the white
range — the Verdelho, Arinto, and field-blend Antão Vaz bottlings
are some of Portugal's most consistent whites at fair prices.

## Quinta do Mouro

A smaller estate near Estremoz known for very long-lived,
old-vine-Aragonez-driven wines. The flagship Quinta do Mouro Reserva
has a strong following among collectors.

## Herdade do Rocim

A modern estate in the Vidigueira sub-zone of southern Alentejo,
known for talha-fermented wines made under the Amphora label —
some of the most respected modern talha wines in Portugal. The
estate hosts the annual Amphora Wine Day.

## Tiago Cabaço

A young-generation Estremoz winemaker producing both modern oak-aged
red blends and lighter, more aromatic styles. The "T" Bago de Ouro
and "T" Series have built a reputation for elegance in a region
known for power.

## Susana Esteban

Portuguese-Spanish winemaker Susana Esteban runs a small estate in
the Portalegre sub-zone of northern Alentejo, at higher elevation
than the heart of the appellation. The wines are noticeably
fresher and more elegant than the broader Alentejo style and have
helped reframe what the region can produce.

## João Portugal Ramos

Founded by the eponymous winemaking consultant who built the modern
Portuguese fine wine industry. The Marquês de Borba range remains a
benchmark for accessible mid-tier Alentejo red and white.

## Visiting Alentejo

Évora, Reguengos, Estremoz, and Vidigueira are the main bases for
exploring. The region is open and well-signposted; most estates
welcome visitors with appointment and many offer lunch on
property. Find them on the [interactive map](/map) — filter to
Alentejo.
