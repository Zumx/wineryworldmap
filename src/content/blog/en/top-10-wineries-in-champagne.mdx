Champagne is two cities, three grapes, and a hundred kilometres of chalk
galleries. Visiting splits cleanly between the polished grandes marques in
Reims and Épernay and the *récoltant-manipulant* growers in the villages.

## 1. Krug, Reims

The benchmark of multi-vintage cuvée champagne; long-aged, oxidative,
small-oak vinifications. Visits are private and arranged through allocation.

## 2. Louis Roederer, Reims

Family-owned house with serious estate vineyards and Cristal — historically
the Tsar's champagne — now a vegan, biodynamic-influenced flagship.

## 3. Pol Roger, Épernay

Winston Churchill's house: a discreet family estate famous for the Cuvée
Sir Winston Churchill, and an unusually consistent non-vintage.

## 4. Bollinger, Aÿ

A grower-house hybrid: estate-grown Pinot Noir, reserve wines in magnum
under cork, and the Vieilles Vignes Françaises from ungrafted vines.

## 5. Veuve Clicquot, Reims

Madame Clicquot's house: yellow-label ubiquity, 24 km of chalk *crayères*
beneath Reims, and one of the most professional visitor programmes in the
region.

## 6. Moët & Chandon, Épernay

The largest house and the home of Dom Pérignon; deep chalk cellars under
Avenue de Champagne and tours that run all day.

## 7. Ruinart, Reims

The oldest established champagne house (1729) and the most distinctive
cellar visit — Gallo-Roman chalk *crayères* listed by UNESCO.

## 8. Salon, Le Mesnil-sur-Oger

A vintage-only Blanc de Blancs from a single grand cru; produced only in
the best years, sister house to Delamotte.

## 9. Jacques Selosse, Avize

The reference grower of modern Champagne — oxidative, *vins de lieu*
vinifications from grand cru Côte des Blancs parcels. Visits are
effectively impossible.

## 10. Egly-Ouriet, Ambonnay

A leading récoltant-manipulant from the Montagne de Reims; Pinot Noir-led
champagnes of unusual depth and long lees ageing.

## How Champagne visits work

The grandes marques in Reims and Épernay run scheduled tours daily and are
the realistic entry to a Champagne trip. The grower estates require
appointments and often introductions. Avenue de Champagne in Épernay
concentrates the largest houses within walking distance; Reims hosts the
listed UNESCO chalk *crayères*.
