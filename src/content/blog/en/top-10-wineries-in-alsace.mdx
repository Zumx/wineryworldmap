Alsace sits on the Vosges foothills, sheltered, dry, and one of the most
geologically varied wine regions in France. Riesling, Gewurztraminer, and
Pinot Gris share the grand cru hillsides; Pinot Blanc and Auxerrois fill
the valley floors. Visiting is easier here than anywhere else in France.

## 1. Domaine Weinbach, Kientzheim

The Faller family estate at the foot of the Schlossberg grand cru. Cuvée
Sainte-Catherine and Cuvée Théo are reference Rieslings; the
Gewurztraminer Quintessence de Grains Nobles is iconic.

## 2. Domaine Zind-Humbrecht, Turckheim

The leading biodynamic estate in Alsace, with single-vineyard wines from
the great grand crus — Clos Saint-Urbain Rangen, Clos Häuserer, Clos
Windsbuhl, Brand.

## 3. Trimbach, Ribeauvillé

The classical Alsace house. Clos Sainte Hune (from the Rosacker grand
cru) is one of the world's great dry Rieslings; Cuvée Frédéric Émile is
its slightly more accessible sibling.

## 4. Hugel, Riquewihr

The family-owned house at the heart of the Riquewihr village. Vendange
Tardive and Sélection de Grains Nobles cuvées set the modern style of
Alsace late-harvest wines.

## 5. Domaine Marcel Deiss, Bergheim

The reference for field-blended grand cru wines — a philosophical
counterpoint to the single-variety Alsace mainstream, biodynamic, and
profoundly site-driven.

## 6. Domaine Marc Kreydenweiss, Andlau

Biodynamic pioneer with grand cru holdings in Kastelberg, Wiebelsberg,
and Moenchberg; some of the longest-aged dry Rieslings in Alsace.

## 7. Domaine Albert Boxler, Niedermorschwihr

A small Niedermorschwihr estate with iconic Brand and Sommerberg grand
cru Rieslings; a benchmark for dry, mineral, structured Alsace.

## 8. Domaine Ostertag, Epfig

A leading natural-leaning Alsace estate with strong biodynamic ethics;
Muenchberg grand cru Pinot Gris and Riesling are flagships.

## 9. Domaine Josmeyer, Wintzenheim

The Meyer family estate — biodynamic, with Brand and Hengst grand cru
holdings and a refined, restrained house style.

## 10. Domaine Schoffit, Colmar

The other major estate with Rangen grand cru holdings; Clos Saint-Théobald
Riesling and Pinot Gris from this volcanic site are the flagships.

## How Alsace visits work

Alsace is the most welcoming of French wine regions. The wine route runs
170 km from Marlenheim south to Thann; tasting rooms are open
spontaneously in most villages. Distances are small, and a single base in
Colmar or Riquewihr covers the whole grand cru spine.
