Cabernet Sauvignon is the world's most planted red grape and the
backbone of Bordeaux's classified château system, Napa's cult
estates, and a handful of single-estate icons on every continent.
The producers below dominate the variety globally.

## Bordeaux Left Bank: the first growths

The historical reference. Château Margaux (Margaux), Lafite
Rothschild, Mouton Rothschild, and Latour (all Pauillac), and
Haut-Brion (Pessac-Léognan) — five wines whose 1855 classification
still shapes Cabernet expectations. Pichon Comtesse, Léoville Las
Cases, and Cos d'Estournel often outperform them.

## Bordeaux Right Bank (mostly Merlot-led, but Cabernet Franc)

Pétrus, Cheval Blanc, Ausone — Right Bank estates whose blends lean
Merlot or Cabernet Franc but belong in any global Bordeaux-blend
conversation.

## Napa Valley

The New World counterpart. Screaming Eagle, Harlan Estate, Opus One,
Dominus, Stag's Leap Wine Cellars, and Inglenook are reference
Napa Cabernets. Mount Eden Vineyards (Santa Cruz Mountains, not
Napa) and Ridge Monte Bello deserve a mention as historic California
references.

## Bolgheri, Tuscany

The Italian answer. Sassicaia (Tenuta San Guido), Ornellaia, Masseto
(though Merlot-only), and Solaia (Antinori, technically Chianti
Classico) are reference Italian Bordeaux blends.

## Stellenbosch, South Africa

Vergelegen (Vergelegen Estate), Meerlust (Rubicon), Kanonkop (Paul
Sauer), Rust en Vrede, and De Toren (Fusion V) are reference Cape
Bordeaux blends.

## Margaret River, Australia

Moss Wood, Vasse Felix, Cape Mentelle, and Cullen produce
Bordeaux-style wines often described as more Bordeaux than Napa
in style.

## Coonawarra, Australia

Wynns Coonawarra Estate (John Riddoch), Penfolds Bin 707, and Yalumba
Caley signal Australian Cabernet from the terra rossa belt.

## Chile

Almaviva (Concha y Toro–Mouton Rothschild joint venture),
Don Melchor (Concha y Toro), Viñedo Chadwick, and Sena are reference
Chilean Cabernets.

## Argentina

Catena Zapata, Bodega Caro (Lafite-Catena), Cheval des Andes (Cheval
Blanc-Terrazas), and Achaval-Ferrer round out the Andean Cabernet
landscape — though Malbec dominates the country.
