Sassicaia is the wine that built the modern Italian fine wine
movement. Made on the Tuscan coast at Tenuta San Guido in Bolgheri,
it is a Cabernet Sauvignon-Cabernet Franc blend with overtly
Bordelais ambition — and the wine that forced Italy's appellation
system to rewrite itself.

## The origin

Marchese Mario Incisa della Rocchetta planted Cabernet Sauvignon
cuttings from Château Lafite Rothschild at Tenuta San Guido in the
1940s. For decades the wine was a private family bottling. In 1968,
his son Nicolò first commercialised it. The wine had no DOC — Tuscany's
appellations did not allow Cabernet — and so it was sold as humble Vino
da Tavola.

## The 1985 vintage

The 1985 Sassicaia, blind-tasted by Decanter in 1992 against 33
Cabernet Sauvignons from around the world, was placed first. The
result shocked the wine establishment and confirmed that Italy could
produce wines worthy of Bordeaux's first growths.

## The Bolgheri DOC

In 1994, Italy created the Bolgheri DOC specifically to accommodate
the Super Tuscan wines that had emerged in the coastal hills.
Sassicaia received its own sub-appellation: Bolgheri Sassicaia DOC —
the only single-property denomination in Italian wine law.

## The wines

- **Sassicaia** — the grand vin, typically 85% Cabernet Sauvignon
and 15% Cabernet Franc, aged 24 months in French oak.
- **Guidalberto** — a second wine introduced in 2000; Cabernet
Sauvignon, Merlot, and Sangiovese in roughly equal proportions.
- **Le Difese** — a third wine introduced later; Cabernet
Sauvignon-Sangiovese, designed as an accessible entry to the estate
style.

## The terroir

Bolgheri sits on the Tuscan coast facing the Tyrrhenian Sea. The
maritime climate is markedly different from the inland hills of
Chianti — warmer, drier, with strong marine influence. Soils are a
mix of clay, limestone, and gravel. The vineyards of Sassicaia run
along the slopes above the small town of Castagneto Carducci.

## Reference vintages

The 1985, 1988, 1990, 1997, 1998, 2001, 2006, 2015, 2016, and 2018
are the modern reference points. Sassicaia ages 20-30 years in
great vintages — extraordinary for an Italian wine that did not
exist commercially before 1968.

## Visiting

Tenuta San Guido does not run public visits. Some specialist
operators can arrange tours; otherwise the wine is best encountered
at quality Italian restaurants and Bolgheri-village enoteche.
