Austria makes some of Europe's most precise dry white wine and a
growing roster of serious reds, yet it sits quietly in the shadow of
its larger neighbours. Its signature grape, Grüner Veltliner, has no
equal anywhere else; its Rieslings rival Germany's; and Burgenland's
Blaufränkisch is one of the great cool-climate reds. Built back from
the 1985 scandal that nearly destroyed the industry, modern Austrian
wine is defined by rigour, transparency and terroir.

## 1. Weingut F.X. Pichler, Wachau

The most celebrated estate of the Wachau, the steep terraced gorge of
the Danube. F.X. Pichler's Grüner Veltliner and Riesling from sites
like Kellerberg and Loibenberg — labelled Smaragd, the Wachau's
ripest category — are powerful, mineral and built to age for decades.

## 2. Weingut Knoll, Wachau

A traditional Wachau estate in Unterloiben, instantly recognisable by
its baroque Saint Urban label. Emmerich Knoll makes restrained,
intensely mineral Riesling and Grüner Veltliner that reward long
cellaring — a benchmark of classical Wachau style.

## 3. Weingut Prager, Wachau

A Weissenkirchen estate famed for racy, crystalline Riesling from the
steep Achleiten and Klaus terraces, alongside fine Grüner Veltliner.
One of the purest expressions of Wachau terroir.

## 4. Domäne Wachau, Dürnstein

The region's leading cooperative and a quality powerhouse, vinifying
fruit from members across the Wachau's named single vineyards
(Rieden) into precise, fairly priced Grüner Veltliner and Riesling.
Proof that a co-op can reach the top tier.

## 5. Weingut Bründlmayer, Kamptal

The Kamptal's reference estate, in Langenlois, working the famous
Heiligenstein and Lamm vineyards. Willi Bründlmayer makes superb
Riesling and Grüner Veltliner plus a serious traditional-method
sparkling Sekt — among Austria's best all-round portfolios.

## 6. Weingut Hirsch, Kamptal

A biodynamic Kamptal estate near Kammern, known for energetic,
transparent Grüner Veltliner and Riesling from Heiligenstein and
Gaisberg. Johannes Hirsch is a leader of Austria's modern,
lower-intervention generation.

## 7. Schloss Gobelsburg, Kamptal

A historic estate with monastic roots near Gumpoldskirchen, run by
Michael Moosbrugger. Its Grüner Veltliner and Riesling — and the
oxidatively aged Tradition bottlings — combine deep history with
contemporary precision.

## 8. Weingut Alzinger, Wachau

A small Wachau estate in Unterloiben making strikingly pure, finely
chiselled Riesling and Grüner Veltliner from top sites like Steinertal
and Loibenberg. A connoisseur's address for elegance over power.

## 9. Weingut Kracher, Burgenland

The world reference for botrytised sweet wine outside France and
Germany, on the shallow Lake Neusiedl in Burgenland. The
Trockenbeerenauslese wines, numbered by style and concentration, are
profound dessert wines of international renown.

## 10. Weingut Moric, Burgenland

Roland Velich's estate in Mittelburgenland, which transformed the
reputation of Blaufränkisch. Working old vines in Lutzmannsburg and
Neckenmarkt, Moric makes structured, ageworthy reds that proved
Austria's red potential to the world.

## Austria's regions and classification

Lower Austria (Niederösterreich) holds the great white regions: the
Wachau, with its own Steinfeder, Federspiel and Smaragd ripeness
categories, plus the neighbouring Kamptal, Kremstal and Wagram.
Burgenland, warmer and on the Hungarian border, is red-wine and
sweet-wine country. The DAC system (Districtus Austriae Controllatus)
ties regional names to specific styles, and the rising use of named
Rieden (single vineyards) brings Burgundian precision to the label.

## Visiting Austrian wine country

The Wachau is the scenic jewel — a UNESCO landscape of terraced vines
above the Danube between Krems and Melk, easily reached from Vienna by
train or river boat. The Kamptal around Langenlois is gentler and
equally rewarding. Burgenland, around Lake Neusiedl, is flat, sunny
and ideal for cycling between cellar doors. Most estates welcome
visitors, and Austrian *Heuriger* taverns offer a relaxed
introduction to the local wine.
