Tignanello is the wine that, together with Sassicaia, founded the
Super Tuscan movement. Produced by the Marchesi Antinori at their
estate of the same name in the Chianti Classico zone south of
Florence, Tignanello broke the Chianti rules by blending Cabernet
Sauvignon and Cabernet Franc with Sangiovese — and aging the wine in
small French barriques rather than the traditional large Slavonian
botti.

## The 1971 vintage

The 1971 Tignanello was the first to include international varieties
in significant proportion. Released as humble Vino da Tavola because
it violated the Chianti DOC rules of the time, it nonetheless
received immediate critical acclaim. Marchese Piero Antinori and
winemaker Giacomo Tachis are credited with the vision.

## The composition

Modern Tignanello is approximately 80% Sangiovese, 15% Cabernet
Sauvignon, and 5% Cabernet Franc. The estate is a single 47-hectare
vineyard at 350-400 metres altitude on the Tignanello hill in the
Chianti Classico heartland, with limestone-rich soils.

## The siblings

The Tignanello estate also produces:

- **Solaia** — 75% Cabernet Sauvignon, 20% Cabernet Franc, 5%
Sangiovese. The prestige red, often considered Tuscany's answer to
the first growths. Vintages 1997, 2001, 2007, 2010, 2015, and 2016
are reference points.
- **Tignanello white** — historic, a small amount of Chardonnay
made on the estate.
- **Marchese Antinori Chianti Classico Riserva** — the more
classical wine from the same property.

## The DOC question

Tignanello was released as Vino da Tavola from 1971 until 1992, when
Italy created the Indicazione Geografica Tipica (IGT) category
specifically to accommodate quality wines that did not fit the DOC
rules. Tignanello is now bottled as Toscana IGT.

## Reference vintages

1971 (the first), 1985, 1990, 1997, 2001, 2007, 2010, 2015, and
2016 are touchstones. The wine ages 15-25 years in great vintages.

## Visiting

The Tignanello estate offers structured visits at the Antinori nel
Chianti Classico winery — a Marco Casamonti-designed building
opened in 2012, set partly underground into the hillside. The
visitor experience is among the best in Tuscany; book ahead.
