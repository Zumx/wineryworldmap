Pinot Noir is the most demanding red grape — thin-skinned, early-
ripening, and ruthless about climate. It only makes great wine in a
narrow band of cool sites. These are the producers most associated
with that pursuit.

## Burgundy: the Côte de Nuits

The historical reference. Domaine de la Romanée-Conti (Vosne-Romanée)
is the mythic estate; Domaine Leroy and Domaine Armand Rousseau the
other names of the absolute summit. Domaine Méo-Camuzet, Domaine
Dujac, and Comte Georges de Vogüé fill the next rank. Effectively
none receive casual visitors.

## Champagne (Blanc de Noirs)

A different reading of Pinot Noir — white from black grapes. Bollinger
(Aÿ) and Egly-Ouriet (Ambonnay) are reference Blanc de Noirs houses;
Cédric Bouchard (Roses de Jeanne) is the cult grower.

## Sonoma Coast and Russian River Valley

The American Pinot benchmark. Williams Selyem, Kistler, and Hirsch
Vineyards are reference producers; Rochioli supplies many of the
great single-vineyard sources. Marcassin (Helen Turley) is the cult
Sonoma Coast bottle.

## Willamette Valley, Oregon

Cooler than Sonoma, slightly more Burgundian in style. The Eyrie
Vineyards (the founder), Domaine Drouhin Oregon, Bergström, Antica
Terra, and Cristom are the reference estates.

## Central Otago, New Zealand

The most extreme cool-climate New World Pinot region — high
altitude, low rainfall, dramatic diurnal range. Felton Road, Burn
Cottage, and Rippon are reference estates.

## Tasmania

A small but fast-growing Pinot Noir region. Tolpuddle Vineyard
(Shaw + Smith), Domaine Simha, and Stefano Lubiana are reference
estates.

## Mornington Peninsula and Yarra Valley, Australia

Cool-climate Victorian Pinot. Bass Phillip (Gippsland — technically
adjacent), Yabby Lake (Mornington), Bindi (Macedon), Mac Forbes
(Yarra), and Giant Steps (Yarra) are reference producers.

## Germany (Spätburgunder)

The most underrated Pinot region in the world. Friedrich Becker
(Pfalz), Bernhard Huber (Baden), Meyer-Näkel (Ahr), and August
Kesseler (Rheingau) make Pinot Noir easily worthy of comparison with
village-level Burgundy.
