The Douro Valley is the oldest demarcated wine region in the world,
established in 1756. Schist terraces, native varieties like Touriga
Nacional and Tinta Roriz, and a long British shipping history make this
a region of two traditions: vintage Port from Vila Nova de Gaia and the
dry red Douro DOC that took off in the 1990s.

## 1. Taylor's, Vila Nova de Gaia / Quinta de Vargellas

The reference vintage Port house, with the Quinta de Vargellas estate
in the Upper Douro. Vintage Ports of extraordinary longevity and the
Quinta de Vargellas Vinha Velha single-vineyard wine.

## 2. Graham's (W. & J. Graham's), Vila Nova de Gaia / Quinta dos Malvedos

Another classical British Port house with Quinta dos Malvedos as the
estate vineyard; the visitor experience in Gaia is among the most
polished in the trade.

## 3. Quinta do Noval, Pinhão

The reference vintage Port estate. The famous Nacional bottling, from
a tiny parcel of ungrafted vines, is among the most legendary Ports
ever made.

## 4. Niepoort, Pinhão

A leading family house with serious vintage Port and a pioneering dry
red Douro programme — Batuta, Charme, and Redoma are reference
contemporary Douro wines.

## 5. Quinta do Crasto, Gouvinhas

The estate that demonstrated the modern dry Douro could compete with
Port. Vinha Maria Teresa and Vinha da Ponte are single-vineyard old-
vine bottlings of great depth.

## 6. Quinta do Vale Meão, Vila Nova de Foz Côa

A historic Upper Douro estate (the original source of Barca-Velha)
now producing Vale Meão under the Olazabal family; an iconic dry
Douro red.

## 7. Casa Ferreirinha, Régua / Quinta da Leda

Producer of Barca-Velha, the wine that essentially founded the modern
dry Douro category; only declared in exceptional years.

## 8. Dirk Niepoort's neighbour — Quinta do Vesuvio, São João da Pesqueira

A single-quinta of the Symington family, producing Quinta do Vesuvio
vintage Port and the Capela single-vineyard wine; lagares still trodden
by foot.

## 9. Sandeman, Vila Nova de Gaia

A historic British Port house in Gaia with one of the best visitor
programmes — costumes, cellars, and a long heritage of vintage Port
under the silhouette mark.

## 10. Quinta do Pôpa, Tabuaço

A modern, family-run quinta on the south bank of the Douro; one of
the new generation producers focused on terroir-driven dry Douro
wines rather than fortified Port.

## Visiting the Douro

Vila Nova de Gaia, across the river from Porto, concentrates the great
Port lodges within a short walk. The Douro Valley itself — Pinhão,
Régua, Foz Côa — is best visited by train along the river or by
chartered boat. Many *quintas* now offer overnight stays.
