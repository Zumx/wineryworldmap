Riesling is the most age-worthy white grape on Earth and the most
honest about terroir. The slate of the Mosel, the gneiss of the
Wachau, the granite of Alsace, the slate of the Clare Valley all
produce strikingly different Rieslings. These producers have built
their reputations on it.

## Germany: the Mosel

The Riesling heartland. Egon Müller (Scharzhof), Joh. Jos. Prüm
(Wehlen), Dr. Loosen (Bernkastel), Selbach-Oster (Zeltingen), and
Willi Schaefer (Graach) are reference Mosel growers. Markus Molitor
spans Mosel and Saar with reference dry bottlings.

## Germany: the Rheingau and Pfalz

A different Riesling — fuller, drier, more orchard-fruited. Robert
Weil (Rheingau), Schloss Johannisberg, Künstler, and Leitz are
Rheingau references; Müller-Catoir, Bürklin-Wolf, and Reichsrat von
Buhl are Pfalz references.

## Germany: the Nahe

A surprisingly geologically diverse region. Dönnhoff (Oberhausen),
Schäfer-Fröhlich (Bockenau), and Emrich-Schönleber (Monzingen) are
reference producers of dry to sweet Riesling at very high quality.

## Austria: Wachau, Kamptal, and Kremstal

Drier, more powerful Riesling. F. X. Pichler, Knoll, Hirtzberger, and
Prager (all Wachau) are reference dry Rieslings; Bründlmayer and
Schloss Gobelsburg in the Kamptal; Salomon Undhof in the Kremstal.

## Alsace

A different idiom again — Grand Cru sites, sometimes drier and more
mineral. Trimbach (Clos Sainte Hune), Domaine Weinbach, Zind-Humbrecht
(Clos Saint-Urbain Rangen), Hugel, and Albert Boxler (Brand,
Sommerberg) are reference Alsace Rieslings.

## Clare Valley and Eden Valley, Australia

Lime-driven, bone-dry Riesling in a uniquely Australian style. Grosset
(Polish Hill, Springvale), Pikes, Jim Barry (Florita), and Mount Horrocks
in the Clare Valley; Pewsey Vale and Henschke in the Eden Valley.

## Washington and the Finger Lakes

Eroica (a Chateau Ste. Michelle–Dr. Loosen joint venture) and Long
Shadows lead the Washington Riesling scene; Hermann J. Wiemer, Dr.
Konstantin Frank, and Bloomer Creek lead the New York Finger Lakes
Riesling renaissance.
