Few wine regions look as strange in the vineyard as Santorini. The
vines are trained into low coiled baskets — *kouloura* — that
shelter the grapes from the unrelenting Aegean wind, often on volcanic
pumice and ash with no usable topsoil. Many of the vines are
ungrafted and centuries old, sitting on root systems that pull
moisture out of fog and sea spray rather than rainfall. The result is
Assyrtiko, one of the most distinctive white wines on earth — salty,
piercing, citric, and built to age in a way few warm-climate whites
can claim.

## Domaine Sigalas

Paris Sigalas is the modern reference of Santorini. The Kavalieros
and 7 Villages bottlings are landmark single-vineyard and
single-village expressions, and the late-release Nykteri and Vinsanto
demonstrate the variety's range from steely-dry to honeyed sweet.

## Hatzidakis Winery

The late Haridimos Hatzidakis was one of the island's most respected
biodynamic pioneers; his family has continued the estate. The
single-vineyard Skitali Assyrtiko is among the most ambitious wines
made on the island.

## Estate Argyros

Founded in 1903 — among the oldest continuously operating estates
on Santorini. Yiannis Argyros holds some of the oldest vines on the
island (Argyros Old Vines Estate is from vines averaging more than
150 years old) and makes a benchmark Vinsanto from sun-dried
Assyrtiko, Aidani, and Athiri.

## Gaia Wines

A joint project of Yiannis Paraskevopoulos and Leon Karatsalos. Their
Thalassitis Assyrtiko, including a submerged-in-sea-amphorae cuvée,
is technically polished and widely exported. The Nemea outpost on
the mainland makes Gaia a multi-region operator.

## Santo Wines

The cooperative — owning a wide network of growers and one of the
most spectacular tasting terraces on the caldera rim above Pyrgos.
The cooperative's Grande Reserve Assyrtiko and Nykteri bottlings show
that scale and quality can coexist on Santorini.

## Domaine Argyros Vinsanto

Worth listing separately for the Vinsanto programme — the long-aged
sweet wine from sun-dried Assyrtiko predates the modern dry style of
the island and remains one of the world's great late-harvest wines.
The 20-year aged bottlings are extraordinary.

## Vassaltis Vineyards

A more recent estate by Yiannis Valambous in Vourvoulos, focused
exclusively on Santorini varieties. The Plethora Assyrtiko and the
Aidani bottling are among the most expressive of the newer
generation.

## Boutari Santorini

The Santorini outpost of the long-running Greek wine group from
Naoussa. Boutari built one of the island's largest modern
wineries in 1989 and has been a consistent producer of well-priced
Assyrtiko alongside the boutique estates.

## Artemis Karamolegos

In Exo Gonia, run by fourth-generation Artemis Karamolegos. The
Mystirio Pure series of single-vineyard Assyrtikos has drawn
critical acclaim, and the estate's restaurant is among the most
serious wine dinners on the island.

## Venetsanos Winery

On the cliff edge at Megalochori, with one of the most dramatic
sunset terraces on Santorini. The winery building (1947) was the
first commercial winery on the island; restored in 2014, it now
produces a refined range of Assyrtiko and traditional Mavrotragano
red.

## Visiting Santorini

Most wineries open afternoons and evenings for tasting; the
caldera-rim estates set up tastings timed for sunset. Pyrgos,
Megalochori, Akrotiri, and Vourvoulos cluster the major wineries
inland from the cliff villages. Find every estate on the
