The Tokaj region in northeast Hungary is the world's oldest classified
wine region (the 1737 royal decree predates Bordeaux's 1855 by more
than a century). Botrytised Aszú is the historical glory; dry Furmint
is the modern revolution. These ten estates lead both.

## 1. Royal Tokaji Wine Company, Mád

The pioneering modern estate that revived Tokaji Aszú after the
communist era. The single-vineyard Aszú bottlings — Mézes Mály, Nyulászó,
Szt. Tamás — are reference points.

## 2. Disznókő, Mezőzombor

A historic first-growth estate now owned by AXA Millésimes (also
Pichon Baron, Quinta do Noval). Aszú of great purity and an
internationally polished visitor experience.

## 3. Tokaj Oremus, Tolcsva

The Vega Sicilia group's Tokaj estate. Aszú, Late Harvest, and
the dry Mandolás Furmint are reference wines; cellars carved into
volcanic tuff.

## 4. Château Pajzos, Sárospatak

A historic Tokaj estate restored in the 1990s; classical Aszú with
clean botrytis character and one of the best visitor cellars in the
region.

## 5. István Szepsy, Mád

The most personal voice in modern Tokaj. Szepsy's single-vineyard
dry Furmints (Úrágya, Szent Tamás, Király) effectively defined the
contemporary dry style.

## 6. Demeter Zoltán, Tokaj

A small grower-vintner producing single-vineyard dry Furmint of
extraordinary precision from old vines on volcanic soil.

## 7. Erzsébet Pince (Prácser Erzsébet), Tokaj

A tiny, deeply respected family estate in the town of Tokaj producing
classic Aszú and dry Furmint in artisanal volumes.

## 8. Patricius, Bodrogkisfalud

A modern estate with single-vineyard dry Furmints and high-quality
Aszú; one of the more accessible producers for visiting.

## 9. Holdvölgy, Mád

A boutique estate with elegant labels and a focus on dry Furmint and
late-harvest sweet wines from the Mád hill; visitor-friendly with
tastings in deep cellars.

## 10. Béres Vineyards & Winery, Erdőbénye

A modern estate built in the 2000s with strong winemaking and one of
the most architecturally striking visitor centres in Tokaj.

## Visiting Tokaj

Tokaj town, Mád, and Tolcsva are the main visiting hubs. The famous
*pince* cellars — narrow vaults cut into volcanic tuff and dripping
with the black Cladosporium mould — are the most atmospheric tasting
rooms in Europe. Many estates run scheduled tours.
