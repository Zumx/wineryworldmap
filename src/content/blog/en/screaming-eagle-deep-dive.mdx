Screaming Eagle is the smallest, most secretive, and most expensive
estate in Napa Valley. Total production is a few hundred cases of a
single Cabernet Sauvignon, made from a hillside vineyard above
Oakville that Jean Phillips bought in 1986. The first commercial
release, in 1992, made the estate's reputation overnight. The single
auction lot at the 2000 Napa Valley Wine Auction — a 6-litre bottle
of the 1992 — sold for $500,000 and became the moment in which
Napa cult wine entered general consciousness.

## Jean Phillips and the discovery

Phillips, a former real-estate agent, bought a 50-acre property
above Oakville with no winemaking experience. The vineyard already
contained 1 acre of Cabernet Sauvignon and a few rows of Sauvignon
Blanc and Merlot among older Chardonnay and Riesling. Phillips
made the deceptively simple decision to bottle the small parcel
of Cabernet as a single wine, ripped out the other varieties to
expand the Cabernet plantings, and hired Heidi Barrett as
consulting winemaker.

## Heidi Barrett and the first release

Barrett, the consulting winemaker at multiple Napa estates, made
the 1992 Screaming Eagle. Released in 1995 to a small mailing
list, the wine was Robert Parker scored 99 points; subsequent
releases regularly scored 98-100. The wine became the defining
"cult Cabernet" of the 1990s — alongside Harlan Estate, Bryant
Family, and Colgin — and established a model that has since
shaped Napa's premium segment.

## The auction moment

At the 2000 Napa Valley Wine Auction, a single 6-litre Imperial of
1992 Screaming Eagle sold for $500,000. The lot, donated by
Phillips herself, became an international news story and
transformed the market's understanding of what Napa could fetch.
The auction price was an outlier — actual mailing-list pricing was
$500 per bottle at the time — but the moment fixed Screaming Eagle
in the public mind.

## The sale to Stan Kroenke

Phillips sold the estate in 2006 to Stan Kroenke (the U.S. sports-
franchise billionaire) and Charles Banks. Kroenke later bought out
Banks's share. Under Kroenke's ownership the estate has expanded
vineyards (with the addition of the neighbouring property to make
the Second Flight Cabernet) and built a new winery facility while
keeping production small.

## The wines today

The Screaming Eagle Cabernet remains the flagship at a few hundred
cases. The Second Flight (a separate Cabernet blend, introduced
2008) and The Flight Sauvignon Blanc (released in tiny quantities)
round out the production. The wines are sold exclusively through
the mailing list. Allocation requires being on the waiting list,
which itself is closed.

## Why the wine commands the price

Two factors. First: the volume is genuinely tiny. Second: the
combination of demand from collectors, the absence of normal
retail distribution, and the auction-secondary market means
prices have detached from the cost of production. A current-
vintage Screaming Eagle that is allocated at perhaps $1,000-
$1,500 per bottle to mailing-list members trades on the
secondary market at $4,000-$5,000 and higher in mature vintages.

## The site

The vineyard sits east of Oakville Road on a gentle hillside above
the valley floor. The soil profile is well-drained and the
exposure provides slightly cooler ripening than the warm Oakville
bench below. The estate has never offered public tours; the only
way to taste the wine is to be allocated bottles or to encounter
them at a high-end restaurant or auction.

## Visiting Napa around Oakville

While Screaming Eagle itself is not accessible, the Oakville
appellation around it is full of approachable producers: Robert
Mondavi at the south end, Opus One opposite, Plumpjack, Far Niente,
and Silver Oak within a few minutes' drive. The Oakville cluster
sits in the most consistently great Cabernet ground in Napa. Find
the visitable estates on the [interactive map](/map) — filter to
Oakville.
