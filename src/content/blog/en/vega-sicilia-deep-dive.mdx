Vega Sicilia is the most prestigious red wine estate in Spain.
Founded in 1864 in the Ribera del Duero — almost 120 years before
the DO existed — the estate's flagship Único is famous for an
unprecedented ten-year programme of barrel ageing before release.

## The estate

Vega Sicilia covers 250 hectares in Valbuena de Duero on the Duero
river. Tinto Fino (the local Tempranillo) dominates with a small
amount of Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Malbec. The estate has
been owned by the Álvarez family since 1982.

## The wines

- **Único** — the flagship. A multi-vintage idea: aged at least ten
years (often longer) in a combination of barriques, foudres, and
bottle before release. Single-vintage. The 1968 is one of the most
celebrated Spanish wines of all time.
- **Único Reserva Especial** — a multi-vintage blend of Único from
several years, considered an alternative interpretation of the
estate.
- **Valbuena 5°** — released after five years of ageing; a more
accessible expression of Vega Sicilia.

## The ageing programme

Único's ten-year programme typically includes:
- 2 years in new American oak.
- 3-5 years in a combination of older oak.
- 1-2 years in larger foudres.
- 3+ years in bottle before release.

The exact regime varies by vintage — some are held back longer.

## Reference vintages

The 1968 is the most legendary Único of all. The 1942, 1953, 1962,
1964, 1970, 1976, 1985, 1990, 1996, 2002, 2009, and 2010 are also
benchmarks. Older vintages are sometimes re-released by the estate
after additional ageing.

## The wider Tempos Vega Sicilia group

The Álvarez family expanded the operation:
- **Alión** — a modern Ribera del Duero label using French oak,
since 1995.
- **Pintia** — a Toro estate, since 2001.
- **Oremus** — a Tokaj estate, since 1993, producing Aszú and dry
Furmint.
- **Macán** — a joint venture with the Benjamin de Rothschild
family in Rioja.

## Visiting

Vega Sicilia does not accept casual visitors. Trade visits and
private tastings can be arranged through allocation channels.
Tempranillo lovers should base in Peñafiel and use the village's
museum and tasting rooms as starting points.
