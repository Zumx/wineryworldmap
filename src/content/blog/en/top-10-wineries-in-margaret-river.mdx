Margaret River is the most isolated of the major Australian wine
regions, sitting on a peninsula in the far southwest of the
continent, three hours' drive from Perth. The climate is mild and
Mediterranean — moderated by the Indian and Southern Oceans on
three sides — and the soils are gravelly loams over granite. The
region was virtually empty of vineyards before the 1960s. Today it
is Australia's premier source of structured, elegant
Cabernet-Bordeaux blends and Chardonnay, and the world's most
remote serious surfing-and-winery destination.

## Vasse Felix

The first winery in Margaret River, founded in 1967 by Dr Tom
Cullity. The Heytesbury Cabernet and Heytesbury Chardonnay
remain reference points; the entry-level Filius bottlings are
honest expressions of the regional style.

## Cape Mentelle

Founded in 1970 by David Hohnen (who later founded Cloudy Bay in
New Zealand), Cape Mentelle is now owned by LVMH but retains a
distinct Margaret River identity. The Cabernet Sauvignon and
Chardonnay are both regional benchmarks.

## Cullen Wines

Founded by Diana and Kevin Cullen in 1971 and now run by their
daughter Vanya Cullen — one of Australia's most respected
winemakers. The estate has been biodynamic for over a decade. The
Diana Madeline Cabernet blend is the flagship.

## Leeuwin Estate

Founded in 1973 by Denis and Tricia Horgan with Robert Mondavi as
consultant. The Art Series Chardonnay, with a different painting
on each vintage's label, is regularly named among Australia's
greatest Chardonnays. The Margaret River Concert Series, held
annually in the vineyard, draws international classical and pop
acts.

## Moss Wood

Founded by Bill and Sandra Pannell in 1969. The Moss Wood Cabernet
Sauvignon is one of the longest-lived Cabernets in Australia. The
estate is small and the wines are produced in modest quantities.

## Voyager Estate

Owned by the Wright family. The Tom Price (red Bordeaux blend) and
Project Chardonnay are at the top of the range; Voyager's vineyards
are now in conversion to biodynamic.

## Pierro

A small, family-owned estate that has been particularly influential
for Chardonnay. The LTC (low-toast Chardonnay) bottling sets the
benchmark for the elegant, unoaked-style fine Chardonnay in the
region.

## Howard Park

Family-owned with vineyards in both Margaret River and the Great
Southern. The Riesling — from Great Southern vineyards — is among
the most respected dry Rieslings in Australia; the Margaret River
Cabernet sits comfortably in the regional top tier.

## Xanadu

Founded in 1977. The Reserve Cabernet Sauvignon and Stevens Road
Chardonnay are both estate flagships; the cellar door and
restaurant make Xanadu one of the more visitor-oriented estates of
the region.

## Woodlands

A small family estate that has built a quiet reputation for very
long-lived Cabernet from old vines. The Eleanor and Nicolas
Cabernets are some of the most age-worthy reds in Western
Australia.

## Visiting Margaret River

The region is laid out along three roads (Caves Road, Bussell
Highway, and Carbunup Road) and is easily explored in two or three
days. Most estates open cellar doors daily. Find them on the
