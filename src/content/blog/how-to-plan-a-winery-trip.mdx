A wine trip is the most appointment-driven and transport-sensitive of all
drinks trips. Plan the bookings and the driver and it is one of the great slow
travels; wing it and you will find locked gates and a licence problem.

## Step 1: Pick one wine region

Wine trips fail when they sprawl. Choose a single region — a Bordeaux
appellation, a Burgundy stretch, a New World valley — and base centrally. Use
the [map](/map) to see how estates cluster and where to stay within reach of
several.

## Step 2: Book appointments early

This is the decisive step. Outside casual New World tasting rooms, most quality
estates — and almost all famous ones — require appointments, sometimes weeks
ahead. The greatest names may not accept visitors at all; plan a realistic mix
of bookable estates and a specialist guide for access.

## Step 3: Solve transport before anything else

Wine tasting and driving do not mix, and wine roads are rural and winding.
Decide, in order: a private wine-tour driver/guide, a regional shuttle, or a
designated non-taster. Lock this before the itinerary — it shapes everything.

## Step 4: Cap it at two or three estates a day

Two well-chosen estates with a vineyard tour and a proper lunch beats four
rushed tastings. Palate fatigue and alcohol both compound; the third or fourth
stop is usually wasted on a beginner and tiring even for enthusiasts.

## Step 5: Sequence the day

Sparkling and whites earlier, big reds and sweet wines later, with a real meal
in the middle. Put the estate you most want to visit first, in case the day
runs long. Build in the village or town — wine regions are beautiful beyond
the cellars.

## Step 6: Plan tasting strategy

Decide up front to spit and hydrate. Carry water, ask for it everywhere, and
treat each tasting as evaluation. This is how you experience six estates over
a trip rather than fading after the first afternoon.

## Step 7: Handle buying and shipping

Decide how wine gets home: estate shipping (often the simplest internationally),
checked-luggage limits, and customs/duty allowances for crossing borders. Buy
on the last day, or have estates ship, rather than carrying cases between
stops.

## Step 8: Respect the estate

Be on time for appointments (a missed booking is a real cost to a small
estate), buy a bottle where you tasted, ask genuine questions, and never arrive
already drunk. Access depends on guests behaving well.

## Put it together

One region, then booked appointments, then the driver, then a capped daily
plan. Open the [map](/map), cluster the estates, lock the transport, and a
winery trip becomes the best kind of unhurried travel.
